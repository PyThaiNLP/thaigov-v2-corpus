PM and spouse chair chanting and alms-giving ceremony
on auspicious occasion of Her Majesty the Queen’s birthday anniversary
June 3, 2025, at 0730hrs, at Sanam Luang Royal Plaza, Bangkok, Government Spokesperson Jirayu Houngsub disclosed that Prime Minister Paetongtarn Shinawatra and spouse, Mr. Pidok Sooksawas, presided over the chanting and alms-giving ceremony on auspicious occasion of Her Majesty the Queen’s birthday anniversary. Attending the ceremony were Privy Councilors and spouses, President of the Supreme Court, President of the Senate (Representative of the President of the National Assembly), President of the Supreme Administrative Court, Chairman of the Election Commission, Secretary General of Office of the Ombudsman, Presidents of constitutional organizations, cabinet members and spouses, officials of royal offices and agencies, Secretary General to the Prime Minister, Army commanders, Commissioner of the Royal Thai Police, heads of government agencies (permanent secretaries or equivalent), and Permanent Secretary to the Bangkok Metropolitan Administration.
Upon arrival, the Prime Minister and spouse lit candle and incense sticks to pay homage to the Triple Gems, and laid the golden offerings in front of Her Majesty’s portrait. Following the chanting by 10 senior monks, the Prime Minister and participants presented the monks with the triple robes and offerings. She, then, paid homage to the Triple Gems, and Her Majesty’s portrait, and led the participants to give alms to 148 monks to end the ceremony.
Later, the Prime Minister and spouse signed a book of blessing for Her Majesty the Queen at the Red Room, the Grand Palace.

Cabinet approves increase of compensation rate for laid off workers
May 6, 2025, Deputy Government Spokesperson Kharom Phonpornklang disclosed that the cabinet has made approval to the draft Ministerial Regulation specifying principle and rate of compensation benefit in case of unemployment (No. …), B.E. …, as proposed by Ministry of Labor and deliberated by Office of Council of State. Ministry of Labor is also instructed to take the comments of Office of National Economic and Social Development Council into consideration.
At present, employees who are no longer insured under Section 33 of the Social Security Act, B.E. 2533 (1990) due to layoff are entitled to receive unemployment compensation in accordance with the Ministerial Regulation specifying principle and rate of compensation benefit in case of unemployment, B.E. 2547 (2004) at a rate of 50% of daily wages, which may not be sufficient for their living expenses under the current economic situation. In order to alleviate the burden of the insured persons, the cabinet, therefore, approved an increase of compensation benefit rate due to layoff to 60% of daily wages for up to 180 days.
Additional expenditure of approximately 1,035.40 million Baht per year is estimated from the increase in compensation benefit. However, Ministry of Labor affirmed that the increase would not affect stability of the Social Security Fund.

Cabinet approves designation of May as “Mind Month”
May 6, 2025, at 1135hrs, at the Command Building I, Government House, following the weekly cabinet meeting, Prime Minister Paetongtarn Shinawatra disclosed that the cabinet has approved allocation of the Government’s FY2025 central fund for emergency and contingency, at the amount of 2,049.69 million Baht, to Ministry of Transport’s Department of Highway and Department of Rural Roads for the restoration of transport infrastructure damaged by flood and other disasters in 17 provinces, namely, Chiang Rai, Mae Hong Son, Chiang Mai, Phayao, Lampang, Phrae, Nan, Uttaradit, Tak, Sukhothai, Loei, Phuket, Yala, Phitsanulok, Udon Thani, Nong Khai, and Kanchanaburi provinces.
The cabinet also approved a proposal of Ministry of Public Health to designate the month of May each year as the “Mind Month”, which will be promoted under the concept of mental health being everyone's business. The Department of Mental Health will collaborate with its network institutions and organizations in 4 main agendas: 1) Stigma; 2) Awareness; 3) How to seek help; and 4) Safe Space.
The Prime Minister also revealed about the campaign “Amazing Thailand Grand Tourism and Sports Year 2025”, which was proposed by the Tourism Policy Committee. The cabinet instructed all concerned agencies to proceed according to the campaign guidelines in a bid to promote tourism as an engine of growth for income generation and economic stimulation at the time of global economic volatility. Focus will be made on synergizing cooperation among partners of all sectors, i.e., airlines, hotels and accommodations, restaurants, tour companies, department stores, shops, public transportation and event management firms.

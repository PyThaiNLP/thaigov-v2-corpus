PM presents flower offerings in front of King Rama I’s statue on Chakri Day
Government Spokesperson Jirayu Houngsub disclosed that on April 6, 2025, at 1700hrs, Prime Minister Paetongtarn Shinawatra presented flower offerings in front of King Rama I’s statue on occasion of the Chakri Day at the statue of King Rama I, Phra Phutta Yodfah Bridge. Also participating in the ceremony were Deputy Minister of Public Health Dej-is Khaothong, as the cabinet’s royal attendance representative, and Prime Minister’s Secretary General Prommin Lertsuridej. The ceremony was held in remembrance of the First King of Chakri Dynasty.
Upon arrival, the Prime Minister bowed and presented 2 sets of flower offerings in the name of the Prime Minister and on behalf of the cabinet members. She, then, bowed again to complete the ceremony.
In 1919, King Rama VI proclaimed April 6 of each year as “King Phra Phutta Yodfah Chulaloke Maharaj Day” in order for the Thai people to express reverence and remember the benevolences of all the Chakri Kings. April 6, 1782 marks the beginning of Chakri Dynasty and Bangkok, as capital of the nation, when King Rama I ascended to the throne.

PM thanks Armenia for supporting initiation of Thailand-EAEU FTA negotiations
January 23, 2025, at 1700hrs (Davos local time), Davos, Swiss Federation, during her attendance at the World Economic Forum Annual Meeting 2025 (WEF AM25), Prime Minister Paetongtarn Shinawatra had a bilateral discussion with Prime Minister Nikol Pashinyan of the Republic of Armenia. Government Spokesperson Jirayu Houngsub disclosed gist of the meeting is as follows:
The Prime Minister was pleased with the relations between the two countries, and thanked Armenia for its support on the initiation of the free trade agreement negotiation between Thailand and the Eurasian Economic Union (Thailand-EAEU FTA), of which Armenia is a member. She was confident that Thailand-EAEU FTA would help increase trade volume and leverage economic potential of both parties.
The Armenian Prime Minister expressed Armenia’s intent to promote closer relations and cooperation with Thailand, and would assign the Armenian Ambassador to Beijing, People’s Republic of China, to include Thailand in the list of countries under its jurisdiction.
Both the Prime Ministers also agreed to push ahead the negotiation on visa exemption between Thailand and Armenia to promote people’s visits and travels. Concerned agencies of both countries will be assigned to proceed with the matter.

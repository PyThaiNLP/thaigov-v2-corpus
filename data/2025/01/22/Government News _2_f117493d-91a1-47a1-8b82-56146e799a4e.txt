PM meets with Coca-Cola CEO during attendance at WEF AM25
January 21, 2025, at 1100hrs (Davos local time), at Grischa DAS Hotel, Davos, Swiss Federation, during her attendance at the World Economic Forum Annual Meeting 2025 (WEF AM25), Prime Minister Paetongtarn Shinawatra met with Mr. James Quincey, Chairman and CEO of the U.S.-based Coca-Cola Company.
The Prime Minister thanked Coca-Cola for its contribution to Thailand’s employment and economic growth through the company’s long-standing business operation in the country. She also commended the company's commitment to promoting sustainable development, with its policy on packaging recycling and water management for sustainable resource utilization.
The Coca-Cola Chairman expressed pleasure to meet with the Prime Minister today, and affirmed the company's confidence in Thailand's economic potential. Coca-Cola stands ready to work together with the Thai Government in enhancing skills and quality of life of the Thai farmers.
The Prime Minister affirmed the Government's policies on economic development and promotion of foreign trade and investment, especially promotion of supply chain and green economy. The Prime Minister also expressed her intention to promote educational opportunities for the future of Thai youths through scholarship provision and skill enhancement, and emphasized the Government’s policy on ease of doing business.
Both parties also discussed Thailand’s enforcement of Sustainable Packaging Management Act, for which Coca-Cola has played a significant role in pushing, and came to terms that there be potential for further cooperation, especially in the areas of water security and soft power promotion which Coca-Cola has expertise.

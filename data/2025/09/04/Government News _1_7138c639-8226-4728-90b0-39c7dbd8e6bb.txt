Construction of border fences along border area in Sakaeo approved by Ad Hoc Center for Thailand-Cambodia Border Situation
September 4, 2025, at 0700hrs, Government Spokesperson Jirayu Houngsub, as member of Ad Hoc Center for Thailand-Cambodia Border Situation, updated overall situation along the Thai -Cambodian border in 7 provinces as of this morning that the situation remains stable with no report of any clash nor violence. Military forces continue to closely monitor the situation and stay vigilant at all times.
The Ad Hoc Center for Thailand-Cambodia Border Situation, in its 31st meeting yesterday, discussed debriefing of the field visit to Chanthaburi and Trat provinces of the IOT on September 2, 2025 to observe security operations, mine clearance, and local people’s wellbeing. The result reveals Thailand’s straightforward and transparent operation, and its commitment to cooperation for the maintenance of regional peace.
Thailand is also preparing for the 2nd Extraordinary GBC (General Border Committee) meeting to be held in Cambodia’s Koh Kong Province, and Trat during September 7–10, 2025, where it will take on a proactive move to clarify the facts regarding landmines to UN high-ranking officials, the International Committee of the Red Cross, and state parties to the Ottawa Treaty.
The meeting also approved the construction of a 16-kilometer fence in the area of boundary markers 50-51 in Sa Kaeo Province to enhance public safety and to prevent Cambodia’s intrusion.

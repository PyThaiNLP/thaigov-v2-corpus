Govt commits to utilizing all available mechanisms to mitigate plights of people affected by Thailand-Cambodia situation
July 27, 2025, Deputy Government Spokesperson Sasikarn Watthanchan disclosed that the Government, through the Department of Disaster Prevention and Mitigation (DPPM), has closely monitored the ongoing Thailand-Cambodia tension along the border area, and ordered local DPPM offices to mobilize disaster response equipment and machineries, as well as personnel, to ensure safety of local residents in all aspects, i.e., evacuation, shelter provision, and basic livelihoods. Initially, ambulances, mobile drinking water and cooking units, chemical foam fire engines, and lighting-generated vehicles are on standby in Chanthaburi, Trat, and Sakaeo, additional areas at risk.If there is an urgent need for evacuation, locals will promptly receive a notification via the National Disaster Warning Center’s cell broadcast system.
The cabinet also approved an increase of emergency funds from 20 million Baht to 100 million Baht per province to be allocated to Surin, Buriram, Ubon Ratchathani, and Sisaket, while those for Chanthaburi, Trat, and Sakaeo are currently awaiting approval.
According to the Deputy Government Spokesperson, the Government commits to utilizing all available mechanisms to their full capacity in a bid to mitigate plights of the people affected by Thailand-Cambodia situation in all dimensions.

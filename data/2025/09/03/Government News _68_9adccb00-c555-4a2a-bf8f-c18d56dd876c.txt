DDPM warns risk areas in 58 provinces to prepare for typhoon “Kajiki”
August 25, 2025, Deputy Government Spokesperson Sasikarn Watthanachan disclosed that the Department of Disaster Prevention and Mitigation has sent weather warning to risk areas in 58 provinces across the country as typhoon “Kajiki” is approaching. During August 24-27, flash floods, runoffs, inundation, and landslides are very likely in several risk areas. The rather strong wind will also force the waves in the upper Andaman Sea and the upper Gulf up to 2-3 meters high and above 3 meters high in thundershowers.
The Department of Disaster Prevention and Mitigation (DDPM) has advised governors in affected provinces to strictly follow the instruction of Minister and Deputy Minister of the Interior. They are also advised to assign officers to closely monitor the weather, rainfall, and water level 24/7, and to warn residents in risk areas well in advance should the situation is likely to worsen. Rapid-response units, disaster relief task forces, and machinery must be prepared for immediate operation. Provincial administrations and regional DDPMs are urged to closely coordinate in facilitating disaster prevention and relief efforts.
Areas expected to be affected by typhoon “Kajiki” for August 24, 2025, are:
Northern Region (10 provinces): Lampang, Phrae, Nan, Uttaradit, Tak, Sukhothai, Kamphaeng Phet, Phitsanulok, Phichit, and Phetchabun
Northeastern Region (10 provinces): Nong Khai, Bueng Kan, Nong Bua Lamphu, Udon Thani, Sakon Nakhon, Nakhon Phanom, Chaiyaphum, Khon Kaen, Kalasin, and Mukdahan
Central Region (9 provinces): Kanchanaburi, Ratchaburi, Suphan Buri, Lop Buri, Nakhon Nayok, Prachin Buri, Chanthaburi, Trat, and Phetchaburi
Southern Region (3 provinces): Ranong, Phang-nga, and Phuket
Bangkok
Areas expected to be affected by typhoon “Kajiki” for August 25, 2025, are:
Northern Region (10 provinces): Chiang Rai, Lamphun, Lampang, Phayao, Phrae, Nan, Uttaradit, Phitsanulok, Phichit, and Phetchabun
Northeastern Region (10 provinces): Loei, Nong Khai, Bueng Kan, Nong Bua Lamphu, Udon Thani, Sakon Nakhon, Nakhon Phanom, Mukdahan, Nakhon Ratchasima, and Ubon Ratchathani
Central Region (15 provinces): Kanchanaburi, Ratchaburi, Nakhon Nayok, Prachin Buri, Sa Kaeo, Chanthaburi, Trat, Phetchaburi, Prachuap Khiri Khan, Pathum Thani, Nonthaburi, Nakhon Pathom, Samut Sakhon, Samut Songkhram, and Samut Prakan
Southern Region (5 provinces): Chumphon, Ranong, Phang-nga, Phuket, and Krabi
Bangkok
Areas expected to be affected by typhoon “Kajiki” for August 26-27, 2025, are:
Northern Region (17 provinces): Mae Hong Son, Chiang Mai, Chiang Rai, Lamphun, Lampang, Phayao, Phrae, Nan, Uttaradit, Tak, Sukhothai, Kamphaeng Phet, Phitsanulok, Phichit, Phetchabun, Nakhon Sawan, and Uthai Thani
Northeastern Region (12 provinces): Loei, Nong Khai, Bueng Kan, Udon Thani, Sakon Nakhon, Nakhon Phanom, Khon Kaen, Kalasin, Mukdahan, Yasothon, Amnat Charoen, and Ubon Ratchathani
Central Region (16 provinces): Kanchanaburi, Ratchaburi, Chai Nat, Nakhon Nayok, Prachin Buri, Sa Kaeo, Chachoengsao, Chon Buri, Rayong, Chanthaburi, Trat, Phetchaburi, and Prachuap Khiri Khan, as well as Pathum Thani, Nonthaburi, and Samut Prakan
Southern Region (4 provinces): Chumphon, Ranong, Phang-nga, and Phuket
Bangkok
Additionally, there is a warning for strong winds and high waves in Chonburi, Rayong, Chanthaburi, Trat, Ranong, and Phang-nga, especially along the coastal areas, islands, and major tourist destinations.
Residents in the risk areas are strongly advised to closely follow weather updates and warnings from the Thai Meteorological Department and DDPM. They may also download the "THAI DISASTER ALERT" application, or seek assistance via LINE: "ปภ.รับแจ้งเหตุ 1784" (Line ID: @1784DDPM), or Hotline 1784 (24/7). The Deputy Government Spokesperson affirmed the Government’s commitment to stand by and assist the people in every situation to ensure their safety.

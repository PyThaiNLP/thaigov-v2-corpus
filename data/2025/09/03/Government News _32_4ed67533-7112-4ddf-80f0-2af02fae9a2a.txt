Thai residents in Cambodia advised to leave country as soon as they can safely do so
July 25, 2025, Deputy Government Spokesperson Sasikarn Watthanachan disclosed about the Government’s concern over wellbeing of Thai nationals working at the border area, and Cambodian migrant workers in Thailand, and warned them to exercise caution in their daily lives and avoid traveling to the areas at risk as safety is the most important matter. The royal Thai embassy in Phnom Penh also advised that Thai nationals currently staying or residing in Cambodia leave the country as soon as they can safely do so, and sought cooperation of Thais not to travel to Cambodia now until the situation improves. In case of emergency or need for assistance, please contact the Royal Thai Embassy in Phnom Penh, emergency phone number: (+855) 975 749 682, Royal Thai Consulate-General in Siem Reap, phone number: (+855) 86 608 999, Call Center Department of Consular Affairs, phone number: (+66) 2 572 8442 at all times. The Government has instructed relevant agencies to closely monitor the ongoing situation between Thailand and Cambodia and stand ready to provide assistance to the people in all aspects.

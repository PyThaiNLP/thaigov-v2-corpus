Suvarnabhumi Airport boasts world’s no. 1 airport with most airlines served
July 23, 2025, at 0830hrs, Deputy Government Spokesperson Sasikarn Watthanachan disclosed about the survey conducted by Brilliant Maps, a website that shares interesting information through informative maps, on top 11 Airports in the World by the number of airlines, according to which Thailand’s Suvarnabhumi Airport stays at the top with 113 airlines, and is followed by:
2: Paris Charles de Gaulle Airport (Paris) – 105 airlines
3: Dubai International Airport – 97 airlines
4: Istanbul International Airport – 93 airlines
5: Rome Leonardo da Vinci–Fiumicino Airport – 92 airlines
6: Milan Malpensa Airport – 86 airlines
7: Shanghai Pudong International Airport – 83 airlines
8: Narita International Airport (Japan) – 83 airlines
9: Singapore Changi Airport – 82 airlines
10: Frankfurt Airport – 80 airlines
This accomplishment is a concrete example of the Government’s continuous effort to develop and improve the nation’s airports to ensure that the passengers receive good travel experiences with convenience, safety, and modernity, and to elevate the Suvarnabhumi Airport as world-class aviation hub and Thailand as regional hub for tourism and aviation.
The Government, through Ministry of Transport and other concerned agencies, commits to push forward and enhance efficiency of the Thai aviation industry, including increasing passenger capacity of Suvarnabhumi Airport. It also targets to push the Airport into top 20 world’s best airports within 5 years.

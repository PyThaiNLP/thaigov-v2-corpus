Thailand to return detained Cambodian soldiers when clashes end
July 30, 2025, at 1600hrs, Government Spokesperson Jirayu Houngsub, as member of Ad Hoc Center for Thailand-Cambodia Border Situation disclosed about 18 Cambodian soldiers having been detained by the Thai Army following a border clash in the Sam Tae area, Kantharalak district, Si Sa Ket province. These soldiers, who were fully armed by the time of confrontation, surrendered without resistance. One injured Khmer soldier was sent to the hospital, while the bodies of 2 deceased soldiers were returned on July 29, 2025.
With its adherence to the rules of engagement, humanitarian principles, and the Law of War, the Thai military has provided the Cambodian soldiers with basic care including clothing, food, water, and medical treatment. As of today (July 30, 2025), the 18 Cambodian soldiers are being detained at a secure location under the control of the 2nd Army Area. These soldiers will be sent to their home country once a permanent ceasefire is fully complied.

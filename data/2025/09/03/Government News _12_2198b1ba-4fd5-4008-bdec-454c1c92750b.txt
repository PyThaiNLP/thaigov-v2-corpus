Thailand's FTA-utilized exports saw an increase in first 4 months of 2025
July 4, 2025, Deputy Government Spokesperson Sasikarn Watthanachan disclosed about Thailand's FTA-utilized exports during the first 4 months of 2025 (January – April) which is amounted to USD28,834.57 million, an increase of 13.46% and accounting for 79.45% of total export value eligible under the FTAs.
Exports under the ASEAN Trade in Goods Agreement (ATIGA) saw the highest volume, with a value of USD10,259.88 million, accounting for 66.34% of total export value eligible under the FTAs; followed by ASEAN-China Agreement (ACFTA), USD7,032.96 million (91.02% of total export value eligible under the FTAs); the ASEAN-India Free Trade Agreement (AIFTA), USD4,475.67 million (84.29% of total export value eligible under the FTAs); Japan-Thailand Economic Partnership Agreement (JTEPA), USD2,062.61 million (75.57% of total export value eligible under the FTAs); and the Thailand-Australia Free Trade Agreement (TAFTA), USD1,850.89 million (59.39% of total export value eligible under the FTAs).
Top 5 products which utilized FTA export privileges the most are:
1. Other vehicles for transport of goods (with diesel or semi-diesel engines) with the total vehicle weight, including loads, not exceeding 5 tons: USD2,112.47 million
2. Platinum, unwrought or in semi-manufactured forms, or in powder form: USD1,655.82 million
3. Blended synthetic and natural rubber: USD1,138.09 million
4. Fresh durian: USD955.57 million
5. Other platinum, unwrought or in semi-manufactured forms, or in powder form: USD760.76 million
Top 5 agricultural and processed agricultural products with the highest FTA utilization value are fresh durian, sugar from sugarcane, prepared chicken, frozen poultry meat, and fresh fruits (rambutan, longan, fresh pomegranate), with a combined value of USD6,898.01 million, or 23.92% of the total utilization value.
Top 5 industrial products are vehicles for transport of goods; platinum, unwrought or in semi-manufactured forms, or in powder form; blended synthetic and natural rubber; other platinum, unwrought or in semi-manufactured forms, or in powder form; and wall-mounted or ceiling-mounted air conditioners, with a combined value of USD21,936.56 million, or 76.08% of the total utilization value.
Of the 12 FTAs, 5 saw an increased utilization: 1. ASEAN-India Free Trade Agreement (exports to India), increased by 159.18%; 2. Regional Comprehensive Economic Partnership (RCEP), increased by 23.59%; 3. ASEAN Trade in Goods Agreement (ATIGA), increased by 8.39%; 4. ASEAN-China Free Trade Agreement (exports to China), increased by 4.91%; and 5. ASEAN-Korea Free Trade Agreement (exports to Korea), increased by 3.86%. The increased FTA-
utilized exports is a result of an increase in Thailand's overall exports, at USD106,789.28 million, an increase from USD93,904.61 million in the same period last year.
According to the Deputy Government Spokesperson, the Government has placed importance on trade promotion and expansion, especially through leveraging FTAs for business and export advantages. FTA utilization will also increase trade opportunities and enhance national competitiveness.

​Thailand ready for GBC meeting
August 7, 2025, Government Spokesperson Jirayu Houngsub, as member of Ad Hoc Center for Thailand-Cambodia Border Situation, disclosed that at 0930hrs (Malaysia local time), Thai Deputy Defense Minister Gen Nattaphon Narkphanit, head of the Thai delegation for the General Border Committee (GBC), paid a courtesy call on Malaysian Prime Minister Anwar Ibrahim upon his arrival in Kuala Lumpur to thank Malaysia, as ASEAN chair, for its important role on regional peacekeeping, and for hosting the GBC meeting, where Thailand will reaffirm its stance on ending conflict with Cambodia by peaceful means, while adhering to the international law and respecting each other’s sovereignty.
Later at 1400hrs (Malaysia local time), the Deputy Defense Minister, together with Thailand’s high-level delegates, i.e., Chief of Defense Forces Gen Songwit Noonpackdee, Chief of Staff of the Royal Thai Army Gen Thongchai Rodyoi, Chief of Staff of the Royal Thai Navy Admiral Pairoj Fuangchan, Chief of Staff of the Royal Thai Air Forces ACM Wachirapon Muangnoi, and representatives from Ministry of Foreign Affairs, Ministry of Interior, and relevant security authorities, is scheduled to attend Thailand – Cambodia GBC meeting which will be observed by representatives of the United States and China.
At the meeting, the two sides will discuss and establish guidelines to resolve border disputes. Key agendas include restoring mutual trust between military forces of both countries, mutually agreeing upon the ceasefire, setting measures to minimize tension in high-risk areas, and protecting civilians, among others. The Deputy Defense Minister reiterated that Thailand’s ultimate goal is to ensure safety and security of people along the border area and to advance toward sustainable restoration of relations between the two countries.
The Government would like to reaffirm that all actions taken by the Thai side are in accordance with the international law and humanitarian principles with no excessive forces or retaliation against civilian targets. Thailand commits to resolve the dispute with dignity and fairness to achieve sustainable cooperation in the future.

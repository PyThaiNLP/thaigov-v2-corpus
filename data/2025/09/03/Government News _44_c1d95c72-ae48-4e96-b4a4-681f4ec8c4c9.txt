The Ad Hoc Center for the Thailand-Cambodia Border Situation Convenes Morning Meeting to Assess Border Situation Following No Clashes
and Sets Direction for GBC Meeting whilst Reviewing Security Precautions Prior to Civilian Return into the Area
On Thursday, 31 July 2025, 08.45 hrs., the Ad Hoc Center for the Thailand-Cambodia Border Situation convened a morning meeting to assess the current border situation, following a period of no clashes. The meeting is aimed at outlining Thailand’s approach to the upcoming General Border Committee (GBC) meeting and evaluating security conditions before setting a timeline for the safe return of displaced civilians to their hometowns.
Mr. Jirayu Houngsub, Government Spokesperson and Committee member of the Ad Hoc Center for the Thailand-Cambodia Border Situation, stated that the 24th meeting of the Ad Hoc Center for the Thailand-Cambodia Border Situation was held today (31 July 2025) at 09:30 AM at the Office of the National Security Council, Government House.
This meeting assessed the latest developments along the Thai-Cambodian border and defined measures for the government’s continued efforts to ensure the safety and well-being of the people. Key discussion points include preparations for the special session of the General Border Committee (GBC), Thailand’s position following the ceasefire negotiations, and measures to ensure civilian safety in the affected areas.
The Ad Hoc Center for the Thailand-Cambodia Border Situation will officially announce the outcomes of the meeting at 12.00 hrs. at the Santi Maitri Building, Government House.

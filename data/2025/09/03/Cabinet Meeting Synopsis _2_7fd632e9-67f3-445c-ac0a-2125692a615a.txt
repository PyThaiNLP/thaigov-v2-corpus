Title: Royal Decree issued under the Revenue Code Regarding Exemption from Revenue Taxes (No. …) B.E. … (Tax measures to promote SMEs’ Digital Transformation)
The cabinet approved in principle the Royal Decree issued under the Revenue Code Regarding Exemption from Revenue Taxes (No. …) B.E. … (Tax measures to promote Tax measures to promote SMEs’ Digital Transformation), as proposed by Ministry of Finance. Ministry of Digital Economy and Society (MDES) is assigned to work with Ministry of Finance to drive forward and create awareness on tax measures to promote SMEs’ Digital Transformation through monitoring and evaluating benefits reaved from this measure, and submitting such information to Ministry of Finance on an annual basis until the end of the implementation of the measure.
Gist
The draft Royal Decree issued under the Revenue Code Regarding Exemption from Revenue Taxes (No. …) B.E. … (Tax measures to promote Tax measures to promote SMEs’ Digital Transformation) is aimed to encourage SME entrepreneurs to adopt digital technologies in their operations and business management to increase competitiveness. It prescribes exemption of corporate income tax for companies or juristic partnerships whose paid-up capital on the last day of the accounting period does not exceed 5 million baht, and whose income from selling goods and providing services in the accounting period does not exceed 30 million baht. Under the Royal Decree, deduction of 2 times the expenses (not exceeding 300,000 baht) for the purchase/services related to computer software, hardware, and smart devices, or digital services that are registered with the Digital Economy Promotion Agency, but excluding computers. This will take effect from the date the cabinet approved in principle to December 31, 2027.
Title: Draft Ministerial Regulation on Thai Industrial Standard for Heavy motor vehicle equipped with possitive ignition engines: safety requirements; emission from engine, level 4, B.E. …
The cabinet approved in principle the draft Ministerial Regulation on Thai Industrial Standard for Heavy motor vehicle equipped with possitive ignition engines: safety requirements; emission from engine, level 4, B.E. …, as proposed by Ministry of Industry.
Gist
The draft Ministerial Regulation on Thai Industrial Standard for Heavy motor vehicle equipped with possitive ignition engines: safety requirements; emission from engine, level 4, B.E. …, prescribes revision of Thai Industrial Standard for Heavy motor vehicle equipped with possitive ignition engines: safety requirements; emission from engine, level 4 under TIS 3044-2563. The revision is made from “mandating safety requirements and emission standards equivalent to Euro 5 for
pollutants, durability of pollution control devices, and on-board diagnostics (OBD) to “mandating safety requirements and emission standards equivalent to Euro 6 for pollutants, durability of pollution control devices, and on-board diagnostics (OBD)” in accordance with the cabinet’s resolution to address the problem of dust and haze pollution. The draft Ministerial Regulation shall take effect from January 1, 2026 onward.
Title: Contribution to Asian Development Fund 14 (ADF 14)
The cabinet approved the following proposals of Ministry of Finance:
1. Approved the draft instrument of Contribution to Asian Development Fund 14 (ADF 14)
2. Authorized Minister of Finance to sign the IOC to affirm Thailand’s contribution to Asian Development Fund 14
Gist
The Instrument of Contribution (IOC) must be sent to Asian Development Bank (ADB) by June 30, 2025 to affirm Thailand’s contribution to Asian Development Fund 14 for the total amount of 91.12 million Baht (after discount). The payment will be divided into 4 installments at 22.78 million Baht each throughout the years 2024-2028.

Govt to explore feasibility on implementation of 20 baht flat-rate mass transit electric train services
October 15, 2024, at 1130hrs, at the Command Building I, Government House, following the weekly cabinet meeting, Prime Minister Paetongtarn Shinawatra disclosed about the Government’s flood relief measures, which include compensation of 9,000 Baht per household, and additional 10,000 Baht per household for mud cleaning, as well as exemption of electricity and water bills for house cleaning. Ministry of Finance will also implement related tax measures, reduction of rental fees for royal land leases, and soft loans to restore houses and residences, and businesses. Detail will later be disclosed by Deputy Prime Minister and Minister of Finance Pichai Chunhavajira.
On another matter, the Prime Minister added that Minister of Transport and Minister of Finance are assigned to work together to explore feasibility on the implementation of the 20 baht flat-rate mass transit electric train services, which is aimed to alleviate people’s transport burden and encourage them to use electric trains for commuting to help reduce pollution. The study will take into account benefits, cost effectiveness, financial sources, and management.

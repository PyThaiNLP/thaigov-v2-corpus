PM instructs Thai embassy in Tel Aviv to evacuate Thai nationals if unrest in Israel escalates
October 2, 2024, Prime Minister’s advisor Jirayu Houngsab disclosed that Prime Minister Paetongtarn Shinawatra expressed concern over the wellbeing of Thai people in Israel, following the current unrest. She instructed Ministry of Foreign Affairs and nearby royal Thai embassy to closely monitor the situation, and notify Thai citizens in the area to immediately evacuate and seek help with concerned authorities should the situation escalates and be too risky.
The Royal Thai Embassy in Tel Aviv issued a notification on September 30, 2024 to strongly advise Thai nationals residing in Metula, Misgav Am, Kfar Giladi to immediately evacuate after the Israel Defense Forces (IDF) declared the near-Lebanon-border communities “closed military zones”, noting that entry to the areas is prohibited. The Thai authorities have also been monitoring the situation 24/7.
Thai nationals who need assistance in evacuating may contact the Royal Thai Embassy in Tel Aviv via phone no. +972 546368150 and +972 503673195 (Consular Affairs), and + 972 9-954-8431, +972 54-469-3476 (Labor Affairs), or LINE id. 0544693476.
The Prime Minister’s advisor added that the Prime Minister is very concerned over the safety of Thai nationals, and ordered the Royal Thai Embassy in Tel Aviv to issue notifications and take actions based on risk levels stipulated in the emergency and evacuation plan. The embassy is to prepare for immediate evacuation of Thai nationals should the situation becomes crisis-ridden. He also urged the Thais in Israel to seek assistance and evacuation facilitation from the authorities if there is a need

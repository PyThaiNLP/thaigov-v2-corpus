​PM orders immediate probe on deaths of 6 foreigners at 5-star hotel
July 16, 2024, at 2120hrs, Prime Minister Srettha Thavisin visited the Grand Hyatt Erawan Bangkok Hotel to follow up on the case of mysterious deaths of 6 foreigners. He was accompanied by national police chief Pol Gen Torsak Sukvimol and chief of Bangkok's Metropolitan Police Bureau Pol Lt. Gen Thiti Sangsawang.
The Prime Minister later gave an interview that the incident was unforeseen and would need further investigation. According to the report, 6 foreigners, 4 Vietnamese and 2 American nationals, was found death at a hotel room. It is assumed the six had been dead for at least 24 hours before their bodies were discovered. Initially, the scene shows no sign of robbery nor assaults. Based on the assumption of the police, there might be another person involved whose identity is still unknown. The investigation is ongoing as autopsy is being conducted to see if they had ingested anything.
The Prime Minister affirmed that the incident would not affect tourism. The Ambassador of Vietnam to Thailand has also been reported on the incident. The chief of Bangkok's Metropolitan Police Bureau will later give a press briefing on the progress of the case.

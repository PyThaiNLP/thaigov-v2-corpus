​Govt Spokesperson: Thailand and Cambodia to resume talk on Overlapping Claims Area in Gulf of Thailand
Government Spokesperson Chai Wacharonke disclosed about an official visit to Thailand of His Excellency Samdech Moha Borvor Thipadei HUN MANET, Prime Minister of the Kingdom of Cambodia, on February 7, 2024 that the issue of overlapping claims area (OCA) in the Gulf of Thailand will be discussed at the meeting between Prime Minister and Minister of Finance Srettha Thavisin and the Cambodian counterpart. A resolution in accordance with the international law is expected to be reached, as the two countries hope to share the benefit of natural resources found in the OCA to elevate quality of life of people, and enhance their energy security. The Prime Minister commits to seek a way out that are acceptable to both Thai and Cambodian people.
According to the Government Spokesperson, the Prime Minister believes that friendship between the two countries and Thailand’s foreign policy to befriend with all the countries would be key factors in reaching an agreement on the issue with Cambodia for mutual interest. The Government Spokesperson added that energy resources found in the OCA would help reduce the country’s power generation cost, thereby, alleviating people’s financial burden and enhancing the country’s competitiveness.

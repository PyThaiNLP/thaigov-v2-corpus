PM orders preparation of transportation for detained Thai fishing crews
PM orders preparation of transportation for detained Thai fishing crews
December 5, 2024, at 1800hrs, Government Spokesperson Jirayu Houngsub disclosed that Prime Minister Paetongtarn Shinawatra received an urgent report from Deputy Prime Minister and Minister of Defense Phumtham Wechayachai and Minister of Foreign Affairs Maris Sangiampongsa on the progress of negotiation protocol to release 4 Thai nationals detained by Myanmar in early morning of November 30, according to which the 4 detainees have been released and are returning to Thailand.
The royal Thai embassy in Yangon reported that the 4 Thais have reached Myanmar’s Koh Song on the night of December 4. Township Border Committee (TBC) will later meet to discuss related protocol and procedure. The Prime Minister has ordered Ministry of Foreign Affairs and Ministry of Defense to prepare transportation to pick the detainees up either at Koh Song or at Ranong’s Port. The most important thing is their safety. Ministry of Public Health has also been instructed to have a group of medical staffs ready for health checkup of the detainees once they arrive in Thailand.

PM: Govt strives to promote “Equality of Opportunity”
December 3, 2024, Prime Minister Paetongtarn Shinawatra delivered a statement on occasion of the International Day of Persons with Disabilities, designated by the United Nations on December 3 of each year. Observance of the Day aims to promote an understanding and improvement of disability issues in both legal, social, and economic aspects, and mobilize support for the dignity, rights and well-being of persons with disabilities.
According to the Prime Minister, “Equality of Opportunity” is what her Government believes in. All policy directives are developed with persons with disabilities and their families in mind in order for them to have access to the rights, economic opportunities, resources, judicial process, public services, and inclusive and proper social security. All concerned sectors and agencies have integrated their work in a bid to eradicate obstacles against freedom and integrity of persons with disabilities. Being disabled will no longer a hindrance.
On occasion of the International Day of Persons with Disabilities, the Prime Minister conveyed her moral support to all the persons with disabilities and their families, and affirmed the Government’s commitment to take good care of them and to ensure that they live well and have equal access to basic welfare. This is in order for the persons with disabilities to be inspired and exercise full potential in living their life happily.

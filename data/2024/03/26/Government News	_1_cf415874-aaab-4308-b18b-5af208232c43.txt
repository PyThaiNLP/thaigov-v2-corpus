PM inspects venue for “Korat Expo 2029”
March 24, 2024, at 1030hrs, at Khok Nong Rangka, Khong district, Nakhon Ratchasima, Prime Minister and Minister of Finance Srettha Thavisi visited the venue of the 2029 International Horticultural Expo or “Korat Expo 2029”. Accompanying the Prime Minister were Minister of Transport Suriya Juangroongruangkit, Minister of Digital Economy and Society Prasert Chantararuangthong, Minister of Tourism and Sports Sudawan Wangsuphakijkosol, Deputy Minister of Finance Julapun Amornvivat, and Deputy Minister of Transport Manaporn Charoensri. The Prime Minister was welcomed by the governor of Nakhon Ratchasima, heads of local administrations and local residents. Deputy Government Spokesperson Kenika Oonjit disclosed the gist as follows:
The Prime Minister expressed his pleasure to meet with the people of Korat once again after his previous trip here a week prior to the general election. The 2029 International Horticultural Expo or “Korat Expo 2029” is a major event that is expected to boost the country’s tourism. The venue must, therefore, be properly chosen.
In its Spring Meeting 2024 in Doha, Qatar on March 6, 2024, the International Association of Horticultural Producers (AIPH) approved the rights for Nakhon Ratchasima to host Korat Expo 2029 to be held during November 10, 2029 and February 28 February 2030. The proposed theme for this A1-level Korat Expo 2029 is “Nature and Greenery: Envisioning the Green Future” which will highlight Thailand’s natural marvels, cultural diversity, and expertise in sustainable horticultural and agricultural technologies. The venue is set at a piece of public land in Khok Nong Rangka, Thepalai subdistrict, Khong district. Out of the area of 678 rai, 523 rai will be used as the exhibition area. Hosting Korat Expo 2029 will upgrade Nakhon Ratchasima province as the hub for horticultural innovation and sustainability and a model city for green innovation.
The 2029 International Horticultural Expo or “Korat Expo 2029” is expected to attract approx. 2.6-4 million visitors, significantly generating revenues to the country.

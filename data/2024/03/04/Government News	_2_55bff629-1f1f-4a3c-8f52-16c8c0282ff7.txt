PM off to Melbourne to attend 2024 ASEAN-Australia Special Summit
March 4, 2024, Government Spokesperson Chai Wacharonke disclosed that at 0830hrs today, Prime Minister and Minister of Finance Srettha Thavisin will leave from Suvarnabhumi International Airport to arrive at Melbourne, the Commonwealth of Australia, at 2125hrs (Melbourne local time). He is scheduled to attend the 2024 ASEAN-Australia Special Summit during March 4-6, 2024 to celebrate 50 years since Australia became ASEAN's first Dialogue Partner.
At the Summit, the Prime Minister will underscore cooperation in the key areas that benefit Thailand, i.e., seamless logistics in investment, infrastructure, and people-to-people connectivity, FTAs capitalization, infrastructure development (seaport, the Landbridge project, and the EEC), digital economy, and visa facilitation, as well as the Green Agenda.
The Prime Minister will also put forward resolutions to various regional and international issues, such as strategic competition, and will affirm Thailand’s position with regard to the Code of Conduct in the South China Sea, humanitarian assistance to Myanmar, and food and health security. He will emphasize Thailand’s constructive role in establishing ASEAN Centre for Public Health Emergencies and Emerging Diseases (ACPHEED), utilizing green innovation to tackle the climate change, and in advancing the investment and green innovation cooperation.
During the 2024 ASEAN-Australia Special Summit, the Prime Minister will also have bilateral meetings with the Prime Ministers of Malaysia, Lao PDR, Australia, and New Zealand. In addition, he will have a number of meetings with executives of world’s leading companies, i.e., Fortescue (metal mining and green energy); Linfox (transportation and logistics); Redflow (zinc bromine flow battery as clean energy storage); ANCA (software development); NextDC (data management); and Hesta (superannuation fund).
The Prime Minister will complete his mission in Melbourne in the evening of March 6 (local time), and will travel to Berlin, Federal Republic of Germany to attend the ITB Berlin 2024. The Government Spokesperson will later release detail of the Prime Minister’s mission in France and Germany.

PM visits Chiang Mai’s emerging tourist attractions
PM visits Chiang Mai’s emerging tourist attractions
Government Spokesperson Chai Wacharonke disclosed that on March 17, 2924, at 1640hrs, Prime Minister and Minister of Finance Srettha Thavisin arrived at Mae Kha Canal, Muang district, Chiang Mai, to observe the province’s effort in developing new tourist attractions and promoting soft power. The Prime Minister listened to the briefing of the Mayor of Chiang Mai Municipality Asanee Buranupakorn before strolling along the Canal, and greeting Thai and foreign tourists, as well as the local people and vendors.
The development of Mae Kha Canal is a joint effort of all concerned local sectors to give the Canal a new look through treating and cleaning up water, and beautifying the landscape. The Canal has now attracted a large number of tourists, and generated revenues to local vendors along the Canal. Chiang Mai Municipality has become the 2nd prize winner at the awarding ceremony, held at the Government House, on November 8, 2024, to honor 2023 Outstanding local government organizations.
Later, the Prime Minister, wearing a yellow polo shirt to commemorate the 6th cycle of His Majesty the King’s birthday anniversary, rode a bicycle along Huay Tueng Thao Reservoir, Mae Rim district. Huay Tueng Thao Reservoir is another popular tourist attraction in Chiang Mai. It was built under the Royal Initiative to tackle water shortage problem in the nearby localities. With its beautiful landscape and view, the reservoir also offers a variety of activities, such as a parachute jump, game zones, paintball, ATV car ride, water activities, to name a few.

PM chairs alms-giving ceremony to commemorate Her Majesty Queen Sirikit the Queen Mother’s birthday anniversary on August 12, 2024
Government Spokesperson Chai Wacharonke disclosed that on August 12, 2024, at 0730hrs, at the Sanam Luang Royal Plaza, Prime Minister Srettha Thavisin and spouse Mrs. Pakpilai Thavisin presided over the alms-giving ceremony to commemorate Her Majesty Queen Sirikit the Queen Mother’s birthday anniversary on August 12, 2024. Participating in the ceremony were president and members of the Privy Council and spouses, the House Speaker, president of the Supreme Court, chiefs of organizations under constitution, members of the cabinet and spouses, staffs of the Royal Offices, Prime Minister’s Secretary General, Commanders-in-chief and spouses, chief of the Royal Thai Police and spouse, permanent secretaries to all Ministries, and private sector representatives.
Upon arrival, the Prime Minister and spouselit candle and incense sticks to pay homage to the Triple Gems, and laid the golden offerings in front of Her Majesty’s portrait. Following the chanting by 10 senior monks, he and the participants presented the monks with the triple robes and offerings.He, then, paid homage to the Triple Gems, and Her Majesty’s portrait, and led the participants to give alms to 193 monks to end the ceremony.

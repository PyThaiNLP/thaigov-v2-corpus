​PM observes 7th rehearsal of Royal Barge Procession for Royal Kathina (robe offering) ceremony
September 19, 2024, at 14.45 hrs, at the Royal Barges National Museum, Tha Wasukri Pier, Bangkok, Prime Minister Paetongtarn Shinawatra observed and inspected preparation of the royal barge procession for this year's Royal Kathina (robe offering) ceremony, to be held at Wat Arun Ratchawararam Ratchawaramahawihan on October 27, 2024. Welcoming the Prime Minister were Commanders-in-Chief of the Royal Thai Navy ADM Adung Phan-iam, and Deputy Chief of Staff for Personnel (RTN) and chair of the Royal Barge Procession Preparation Committee VADM Wijit Tanprapha. After being briefed on the procession preparation and related progress, the Prime Minister traveled to Wat Arun Ratchawararam Ratchawaramahawihan to observe the protocol for receiving His Majesty the King and Her Majesty the Queen, and 7th rehearsal of the procession.
The Royal Thai Navy has organized 10 minor rehearsals of the barge procession along the Chao Phraya River on August 1, 8, 15, 22; September 3, 12, 19, 26; and October 3 and 10, and two full-dress rehearsals on October 15 and 22. The public are allowed to witness the rehearsals at the area under Rama VIII Bridge, Thonburi Naval Dockyard, and the Royal Thai Naval Institute to demonstrate their profound reverence to the monarchy. Their attendance also helps promote the country’s tourism.
The Prime Minister commended the oarsmen who are extremely crucial in the royal barge procession, and called for them to do their best for the royal barge procession for the Royal Kathina (robe offering) ceremony to best fit the royal honor.

PM thanks military for dispatching personnel and equipment to help flood-affected people
September 18, 2024, Prime Minister Paetongtarn Shinawatra commended and thanked the military for dispatching its personnel and equipment to help flood-affected people. This proves that the military is always there for the people, never abandoning them.
The Prime Minister was reported that the military personnel have been dispatched to the fields from the very beginning of the disaster until the situation improved, both in Chiang Rai, Nan, Nong Khai, Loei, Bueng Kan, Nakhon Phanom, Ratchaburi, Ubon Ratchathani, and Kalasin provinces. What they have done are such as: 1) Helping local residents clear up accumulated sediments (Ban Lao Liu, Mae Salong Nai Subdistrict, Mae Fah Luang District, Chiang Rai Province); 2) Patrolling in flood-affected areas to prevent crime, monitor safety, and take care of people's property (Mae Sai, Chiang Rai); 3) Installing temporary water treatment system to supply water in 4 locations in Chiang Rai; 4) Repairing/renovating school buildings damaged by flood (Ban Pon School, Pon Subdistrict, Thung Chang District, Nan Province); and 5) Assisting people in Nong Khai province to fill sandbags and reinforce the barriers along the Mekong River (Ban Pak Mang, Village No. 12, Kong Nang Subdistrict, Tha Bo District, Nong Khai Province).
The Prime Minister has given her moral support to the military and its personnel who have worked tirelessly in flood-affected areas. She urged them to prioritize safety and exercise utmost caution in their operations.
The people who have been affected and need immediate assistance or support may contact military forces in their respective area. They may also call Ministry of Defense Disaster Relief Centre 24/7-- Central area and Bangkok: call 02-280-3977, First Army Area (central region): call 02-280-3977, Second Army Area (northeastern region): call 044-245-946, Third Army Area (northern region): call 055-242-859, and Fourth Army Area (southern region): call 075-383-405.

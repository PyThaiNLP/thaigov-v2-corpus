PM: Central budget ready to be allocated for flood relief operation
September 13, 2024, at 0920hrs, at the Royal Thai Air Force Airport (Wing 6), Bangkok, Prime Minisfter Paetongtarn Shinawatra, together with Deputy Prime Minister and Minister of Defense Phumtham Wechayachai, Deputy Prime Minister and Minister of Transport Suriya Jungrungreangkit, Deputy Minister of Defense General Nattaphon Narkphanit, Deputy Minister of Interior Theerarat Samrejvanich, and Prime Minister’s Secretary General Prommin Lertsuridej, left for Chiang Rai’s Mae Fah Luang Airport to follow up on flood situation in the province.
Prior to the departure, Air Chief Marshal Panpakdee Pattanakul, Chief of the Royal Thai Air Force, led the Prime Minister to observe flood relief equipment, such as flat-bottomed boats and rubber boats, which will be sent to Chiang Rai to help flood-affected victims, and to meet with RTAF personnel who will be additionally dispatched to the province for the rescue operation. The Prime Minister thanked RTAF and all agencies for joining forces in helping the people, and affirmed that central budget will be promptly allocated for relief operations.
Concerned agencies are ordered to immediately provide remedies, without having to wait until the flood situation subsides, to alleviate people’s plights. The military is also instructed to urgently rescue and help residents in the higher areas and in the mountains who have been isolated by flood. Cleaning up after flood should be undertaken immediately in the areas where flood water has started to decrease in a bid for the situation to return to normalcy the soonest.
The Prime Minister was also updated on the overall situation and rescue operation by Deputy Secretary General of Office of National Water Resources Chayan Muangsong and Director General of the Department of Disaster Prevention and Mitigation Chaiwat Chuntirapong en route to Chiang Rai.

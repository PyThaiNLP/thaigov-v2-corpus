PM’s schedule for 1/2024 cabinet retreat in Chiang Mai and Chiang Rai
November 23, 2024, Government Spokesperson Jirayu Houngsub disclosed that Prime Minister Paetongtarn Shinawatra is scheduled to travel to Chiang Mai and Chiang Rai provinces during November 28 – December 1, 2024 for the 1/2024 mobile cabinet meeting. Consequently, the weekly cabinet for the upcoming week will be changed from November 26 (Tue) to November 29 (Fri). The Government Spokesperson also disclosed the Prime Minister’s schedule for the field visit as follows:
November 28
20:00 The Prime Minister and delegation leave from the Royal Thai Air Force Airport (Wing 6), Don Muang to Chiang Mai.
November 29
10:00  The Prime Minister chairs the 1/2024 mobile cabinet meeting at Chiang Mai Rajabhat University (Mae Rim campus)
13:30   travels to 700th Anniversary Chiang Mai Stadium, Mae Rim district, to hand down the policy and guideline on prevention and solution to haze and PM 2.5 particulate problems attends the “Northern Coffee Gathering 2024” at OLD Chiang Mai Cultural Center -chairs the launching ceremony of “CHARMING Chiang Mai 2025” at Chiang Mai provincial administrative office.
November 30
09:45 The Prime Minister visits Huai Hong Khrai Royal Development Study Center and chairs a launching ceremony of the project on “Thai people are free from non-communicable diseases (NCDs)” at the International Convention and Exhibition Centre Commemorating His Majesty's 7th Cycle Birthday Anniversary, Muang district Chiang Mai province. Chairs a meeting to follow up on narcotic drugs problem.
December 1
The Prime Minister attends the 1/2024 meeting of financial authorities to discuss rehabilitation of local economy and flood relief through tax measures at Mae Sai Customs Checkpoint, Mae Sai District, Chiang Rai Follows up on the river-dredging plan for flood prevention in Chiang Rai Meets with local people, and hands over national IDs to people with registration status issues at Chiang Rai Greater Mekong Subregion Convention Center Departs for Bangkok.
According to the Government Spokesperson, the Prime Minister’s missions during her field visit to Chiang Mai and Chiang Rai provinces, aside from chairing the 1/2024 cabinet retreat, include following up on various problems, especially haze and PM 2.5 particulates, and narcotic drugs. Post-flood tourism promotion is also in the agenda to boost confidence of the locals and tourists, thereby, stimulating local economy and ensuring equitable distribution of revenue to the people.

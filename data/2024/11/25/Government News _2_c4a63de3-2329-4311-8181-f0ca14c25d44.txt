Foreign investment in EEC increases by 128% during first 10 months of 2024
November 24, 2024, at 0930hrs, Deputy Government Spokesperson Sasikarn Wattanachan disclosed about a report of Ministry of Commerce’s Department of Business Development on inward foreign investments in the Eastern Economic Corridor (EEC) during the first 10 months of 2024, according to which, there are 251 investors, accounting for 32% of foreign investors granted permits this year, an increase of 128%. Total investment value is reported at 45,739 million Baht (28% of the total foreign investment value), an increase of 146%.
Japan tops the list with 86 investors, with the investment value of 16,184 million Baht, followed by China with 59 investors (total investment value of 8,030 million Baht), and Hong Kong with 18 investors (total investment value of 5,219 million Baht). Other countries round up the list at 88 investors, with the total investment of 16,306 million Baht. According to the Deputy Government Spokesperson, most of the investments are in engineering services; automotive parts design services; machinery and equipment installation, testing, repair, maintenance and training services; software services; and OEMs.
In the first 10 months of 2024, business operation permits have been granted to 786 foreign investors, comprising, 1) 181 investments through license application under the Foreign Business Act, B.E. 2542 (1999); and 2) 605 investments through foreign business license application under the Investment Promotion Act, Industrial Estate Authority of Thailand Act, or the international treaties or agreements. Total investment is 161,169 million Baht, creating employment for 3,037 Thai people.
Top 5 foreign investments were from Japan (211 investors, accounting for 27%, with the investment value of 91,700 million Baht), Singapore (110 investors, accounting for 14%, with the investment value of 14,779 million Baht), China (103 investors accounting for 13%, with the investment value of 13,806 million Baht), the U.S. (103 investors, accounting for 13%, with the investment value of 4,552 million Baht), and Hong Kong (57 investors, accounting for 7%, with the investment value of 14,461 million Baht).

PM chairs Royal Kathina Ceremony sponsored by Office of the Prime Minister
November 8, 2024, at 1400hrs, Government Spokesperson Jirayu Houngsub disclosed that Prime Minister Paetongtarn Shinawatra presided over the 2024 Royal Kathina Ceremony sponsored by Office of the Prime Minister at Wat Phra That Choeng Chum Worawihan, Muang district, Sakon Nakhon province. Participated in the ceremony were Deputy Prime Minister and Minister of Defense Phumtham Wechayachai, Deputy Prime Minister and Minister of Digital Economy and Society Prasert Jantararuangtong, Minister Attached to the Prime Minister’s Office Chousak Sirinil, Minister Attached to the Prime Minister’s Office Jiraporn Sindhuprai, Minister of Culture Sudawan Wangsuphakitkosol, Deputy Minister of Transport Manaporn Charoensri, Deputy Minister of Interior Theerarat Samrejvanich, as well as heads of public agencies, government officials, and the public.
Upon arrival at the temple, the Prime Minister paid homage to His Majesty the King’s portrait, and picked up the royally-bestowed Kathina robes before walking into the temple hall. The Prime Minister presented the monks with the Kathina robes, and, together with cabinet members and chiefs of public agencies, offered the monks with Kathina Dana and alms. Donation for the almsgiving in the 2024 royal Kathina Ceremony of Office of the Prime Minister totaled 12,731,219.42 Baht.
Following the ceremony, the Prime Minister paid homage to the province's most sacred Buddha image, Luang Pho Ong Saen housed at the temple. She, then, visited an exhibition on wax castle festival, one of the most elaborate religious festivals in the Northeast of Thailand, and on Sakon Nakhon’s fabric soft power “Don Goy Model”, before greeting local residents.

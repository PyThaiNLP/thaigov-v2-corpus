​PM’s phone conversation with Prime Minister Modi of India
June 6, 2024, at 1400hrs, at Thai Khu Fah Building, Government House, Prime Minister Srettha Thavisin had a phone conversation with Prime Minister Narendra Modi of the Republic of India to congratulate the latter’s winning of the re-election as Indian Prime Minister. Government Spokesperson Chai Wacharonke disclosed gist of the phone conversation as follows:
The Prime Minister congratulated Prime Minister Modi’s 3rd consecutive term as the prime minister of India. He commended Prime Minister Modi’s popularity among the Indians, and affirmed Thailand’s readiness to work together with India to strengthen relations and cooperation.
As this was their first conversation, the Indian Prime Minister also congratulated the Prime Minister for his premiership, and expressed confidence that Thailand and India would continue to forge close relations into the future.
The Prime Minister also mentioned his official visit to India, scheduled later this year, to further reinforce cooperation, both at the bilateral and regional levels, as well as within the BIMSTEC framework. He also hoped for the elevation of relations between Thailand and India to strategic partnership.
The Indian Prime Minister expressed pleasure to welcome the Prime Minister to India at the soonest opportunity as agreed by both parties, and affirmed his attendance at the BIMSTEC summit to be held in Thailand in September 2024 since this would be a good opportunity to advance economic relations and cooperation.

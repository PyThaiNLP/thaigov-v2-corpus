PM orders integrated effort to prevent and alleviate drought disaster
May 3, 2024, Government Spokesperson Chai Wacharonke disclosed that Prime Minister Srettha Thavisin has great concern over widespread impact of drought disaster on all sectors, be it, the agricultural sector, people’s wellbeing, investment, and tourism, especially in the Southern region. He has made an order for all concerned agencies to plan for responses, mitigation, and assistance in a tangible and systematic manner. Ministry of Agriculture and Cooperatives is responsible for water management. Ministry of Interior’s task is to mitigate disaster, whereas the military will be deployed to assist the people in the drought-affected areas. Other concerned sectors are also called on to take part in alleviating people’s plights and ensuring sufficiency of consumption water in all areas.
Relevant agencies have started mobilizing and implementing related measures to tackle the situation and ensure the least impact of drought against the farmers and people. The measures include provision of mobile consumption water and water sources, waterway dredging, and maintenance of public water utilities, among others.
According to the Government Spokesperson, with the ongoing scorching heatwave, the Prime Minister wanted to plan ahead to inclusively tackle drought disaster. He has closely followed up on the situation, and ordered concerned agencies to report to him regularly in order to adjust the plan as appropriate.

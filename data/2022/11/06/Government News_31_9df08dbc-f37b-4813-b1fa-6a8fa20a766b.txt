Public warned of very heavy rain from Tropical Typhoon “NORU” during Sep 28- Oct 1
September 26, 2022, Deputy Secretary-General to the Prime Minister and Acting Government Spokesperson Anucha Burapachaisri disclosed about Meteorological Department’s weather warning on Tropical Typhoon “NORU”, which is expected to make landfall over middle Vietnam on September 28. Coupling with the strengthening of southwest monsoon which is prevailing over the Andaman Sea, Thailand and the Gulf of Thailand, heavy to very heavy rains are likely in the North, the Northeast, the Central including Bangkok and its vicinity, the East, and the South regions during September 28 – October 1, 2022.
According to the Government Spokesperson, people in those regions should take precaution against the severe conditions that may cause flash floods and overflows, especially along the waterways near foothills and lowlands. With the strengthening wind and waves in the Andaman Sea and the Gulf of Thailand, all ships should proceed with caution and keep off thundershowers. Small boats in the Andaman Sea and the upper Gulf area are strongly advised to keep ashore during September 27  – October 1.
The public may follow up on the Meteorological Department’s weather warnings on its website: https://www.tmd.go.th/, or they can make a phone inquiry 24/7 at 02-399-4012 to 13, and 1182.

Deputy PM Prawit Wongsuwon hands down additional policies and measures to tackle flood situation
September 7, 2022, Deputy Government Spokesperson Tipanan Sirichana disclosed that Deputy Prime Minister and Acting Prime Minister Gen. Prawit Wongsuwon, in his capacity as chair of National Water Command Center (NWCC), expressed concern over the impact of heavy rainfall against the people, and ordered for an integrative and proactive operation of all NWCC concerned agencies in accordance with the earlier-announced 13 measures for rainy season.
The Deputy Prime Minister also handed down the following measures in addition to the 13 measures for rainy season:
For Bangkok and vicinity, assign coordinators from concerned agencies to liaise and work closely with Bangkok Metropolitan Administration in a bid to provide prompt and timely assistance to the people
Clearing water obstruction in Bang Khu Wieng canal in Bangkok’s Thawi Watthana district, and coordinating with Nonthaburi provincial administration to open drainage channels
Closely monitoring and properly managing drainage system at water gates to prevent damage against the people living along the rivers and canals.
Continually reporting rainfall, and dam capacity and drainage in a bid to facilitate operation of all concerned agencies
Evaluating, 24/7, water situation from all sources, including rainfall, dam water volume, Chao Phraya River water level, and rising sea level
Ensuring efficiency of flood sandbag walls, improving and strengthening levees and barriers
Immediately notifying the Deputy Prime Minister in case of emergency or urgency

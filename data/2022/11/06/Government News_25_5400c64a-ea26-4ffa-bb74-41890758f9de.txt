​COVID-19 removed from list of prohibited diseases for aliens entering or having residency in Thailand
September 20, 2022, Deputy Government Spokesperson Ratchada Thanadirek disclosed that the cabinet approved the draft Interior Ministerial Regulation prescribing prohibited diseases for aliens entering or having residency in the Kingdom.
The draft Ministerial Regulation is a revision to the Regulation of Ministry of Interior on prohibited diseases for aliens entering the Kingdom or having residency in the Kingdom, B.E. 2563 (2020), according to which COVID-19 has been removed from the list of prohibited diseases prescribed under Sections 12 (4) and 44 (2) of the Immigration Act, B.E. 2522. The draft Interior Ministerial Regulation prescribing prohibited diseases for aliens entering the Kingdom or having residency in the Kingdom will take effect the day after it being announced in the Royal Gazette.
Other prohibited diseases, prescribed under Sections 12 (4) of the Immigration Act, B.E. 2522, include 1) Leprosy; 2) TB at dangerous stage; 3) Lymphatic filariasis (elephantiasis); 4) Drug addiction; and 5) Stage-3 syphilis, and those prescribed under Sections 44 (2) are: 1) Leprosy; 2) TB at dangerous stage; 3) Lymphatic filariasis (elephantiasis); 4) Drug addiction; 5) Alcoholism; and 6) Stage-3 syphilis. These diseases remain on the list of prohibited diseases for aliens entering the Kingdom or having residency in the Kingdom.

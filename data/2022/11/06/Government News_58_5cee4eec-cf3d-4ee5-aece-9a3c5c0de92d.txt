PM’s statement on United Nations Day
October 24, 2022, at 0730hrs, Prime Minister and Defense Minister Gen. Prayut Chan-o-cha delivered a statement via the Radio Thailand on occasion of the 2022 United Nations Day. Deputy Secretary-General to the Prime Minister and Acting Government Spokesperson Anucha Burapachaisri disclosed gist of his statement as follows:
On October 24 of each year, the world celebrates the United Nations Day, of which Thailand has become an active and constructive member for 75 years since 1946. According to the Prime Minister, international cooperation is essential amidst wide-ranging global challenges, be it, economic crisis, food and energy shortage, international conflicts, and natural disaster, among others.
Thailand adheres to UN-centered multilateralism, and stands ready to work together with the global community to utilize new generation’s power and creativity for the creation of the better future. The Prime Minister expressed belief that global solidarity under the UN framework would lead everyone out of all the challenges and promote balanced coexistence.
The Thai Government commits to actively support the missions of UN and other international organizations. The country has presented its candidacy for the UN Human Rights Council membership for the term 2025-2027, and takes pride in hosting several UN and UN-affiliated agencies / regional headquarters in Bangkok. The Prime Minister emphasized that world peace would be realized through international cooperation, and congratulated the 75th anniversary of UN establishment.

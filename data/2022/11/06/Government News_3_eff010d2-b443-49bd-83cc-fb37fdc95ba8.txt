​Govt Spokesperson: 12 Resolutions reached at successfully-held HLM12
August 30, 2022, Deputy Secretary-General to the Prime Minister and Acting Government Spokesperson Anucha Burapachaisri disclosed about the success of the 12th APEC High-Level Meeting on Health and Economy (HLM12), held in Bangkok, Thailand during August 25-26, 2022 under theme: “Open to Partnership, Connect with the World. Balance Health and the Economy”, where 12 resolutions have been reached. At the meeting, Thailand has been praised by the international community for its effective response to COVID-19.
According to the Government Spokesperson, consensus on the 12 resolutions has been reached to create economic and health balances, which is in line with APEC 2022’s overarching theme of "Open. Connect. Balance.", and Thailand’s Bio-Circular-Green (BCG) economic model. The 12 resolutions include promoting international and regional collaboration, and partnerships to improve the health and wealth of people in the Asia Pacific; reopening borders for safe and seamless travel; transferring vaccine production technologies; promoting innovative and digital health technologies; strengthening the public health workforce and universal health coverage; enhancing access to vaccines and other important aspects of global health security; accelerating COVID-19 vaccine delivery; supporting equitable access to safe, effective, quality-assured and affordable COVID-19 vaccines, therapeutics, and diagnostics; increasing health equity and addressing health-related barriers specific to women in the workforce, business, and entrepreneurship; promoting inclusive economic recovery from COVID-19, especially the resumption of normal education, business and social activities in APEC economies; enhancing investments in health security; and create an open, fair and inclusive digital economy, among others.
The Thai Government has placed priority on promoting health security and good healthcare system as a preparation against future health-related threats, and strives to work together with other APEC economies to improve health and wellbeing to all, which have direct impacts on economic benefits and growth in the region.

​Thailand and EU sign PCA to enhance political dialogue on issues of global concern
September 9, 2022, Deputy Secretary-General to the Prime Minister and Acting Government Spokesperson Anucha Burapachaisri disclosed the Government’s appreciation over the hard work of concerned agencies on the finalization of the text of Partnership and Cooperation Agreement (PCA) between Thailand and the European Union (EU).
On September 2, 2022, in Brussels, Belgium, Ministry of Foreign Affairs of the Kingdom of Thailand and EU’s Ministry for Foreign Affairs have signed Partnership and Cooperation Agreement (PCA), which will enhance the political dialogue on issues of global concern and will give more scope for mutually-beneficial cooperation in a wide number of policy areas, including environment, energy, climate change, transport, science and technology, trade, employment and social affairs, human rights, education, agriculture, non-proliferation, counter-terrorism, the fight against corruption and organized crime, migration and culture. It will be a roadmap, which will positively frame EU - Thai relations in the years to come.
According to the Government Spokesperson, the Government strives to expand cooperation with other countries and integrated countries/regions in all dimensions for national growth and competitiveness enhancement, and economic recovery.

PM chairs 6/2022 BOI meeting to deliberate 5-Year Investment Strategy (2023-2027)
November 3, 2022, at 10.30 hrs, Prime Minister and Defense Minister Gen. Prayut Chan-o-cha presided over the 6/2022 meeting of the Board of Investment (BOI) where implementation of the 5-Year Investment Strategy (2023-2027) and investment measures under the 5-Year Investment Strategy (2023-2027) was deliberated. Deputy Secretary-General to the Prime Minister and Acting Government Spokesperson Anucha Burapachaisri disclosed gist of the Prime Minister’s briefing and instruction at the meeting as follows:
The Prime Minister reiterated the importance of the 5-Year Investment Strategy (2023-2027) which would help restructure Thai economy into the “New Economy”, and instructed all concerned agencies to work in an integrative manner to translate the investment strategy into concrete outcomes, while the Government stands ready to provide support. Empowerment of SMEs, startups, and community enterprises should also be focused in line with area-based potentials to ensure widespread growth. He also emphasized the importance of both proactive and reactive implementation of the 5-Year Investment Strategy (2023-2027), with regular follow up and assessment, for the best interest of the country and people.
According to the Prime Minister, investment promotion measures and related operation must be in line with the global agenda of climate action, as he has underscored, at the World Leaders Summit of the United Nations Framework Convention on Climate Change Conference of the Parties (COP26), the utmost importance that Thailand attaches to the problem of climate change, and the country’s readiness to collaborate with all countries and sectors to reach the world’s common goal. Thailand has become one of the leading countries in the world to act aggressively in addressing the climate change challenges, and drive forward the transition to clean energy. Promotion of EVs, for instance, has been well cooperated by all sectors including the private sector.
The Prime Minister emphasized that development of both the old and the new must be undertaken to produces continuous growth and advancement, in line with the targeted poverty eradication in 5 dimensions, namely, health, wellbeing, education, income, and access to public services. This approach is relevant with the Government’s policy to help and take care of all groups of people without leaving anyone behind.
The Prime Minister instructed concerned agencies to ensure efficiency of air transport and air traffic management in a bid to accommodate an increase of foreign travelers during the high season. He also thanked everyone for their effort and dedication to work toward concrete outcomes.

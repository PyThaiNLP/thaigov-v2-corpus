PM content with COVID-19 situation which shows sign of decline
January 19, 2022, Government Spokesperson Thanakorn Wangboonkongchana disclosed that Prime Minister and Defense Minister Gen. Prayut Chan-o-cha is content with the current COVID-19 situation, which is stable, and shows sign of decline. Nevertheless, Omicron variant spreads at a fast speed, and everyone is urged to keep their guard high and continue to strictly follow the public health related measures. Patients with mild symptoms are advised to undergo home isolation and community isolation. The Government Spokesperson also reaffirmed that Thai public healthcare system is efficient enough to cope with the COVID-19 situation.
Today (January 19), a total of 7,122 new confirmed cases is reported, out of which 6,950 were found domestically, and 172 from overseas traveling. The total of 12 deaths and 7,460 recoveries were reported with 81,602 active cases. From January 1, 2022, cumulative number of cases are 121,498, and cumulative number of recoveries, 72,869. Cumulative number of vaccinations across the country from February 28, 2021 to January 17, 2022 is 110,663,601 doses (1st shot: 51,831,768 persons, 2nd shot: 47,676,337 persons, 3rd shot 10,507,085 persons, and 4th shot: 648,393 persons).
According to the Government Spokesperson, the Prime Minister would like people’s life to return to normalcy the soonest. Center for COVID-19 Situation Administration (CCSA) will assess the situation again tomorrow (January 20), and consider readjustment/easing of related measures to be in line with the current situation while taking into account people’s safety.

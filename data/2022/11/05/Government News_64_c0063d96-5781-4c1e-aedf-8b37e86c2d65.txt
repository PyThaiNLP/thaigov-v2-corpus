Cabinet approves purchase of additional 10 million doses of Pfizer vaccines
August 17, 2021, at the weekly cabinet meeting, the cabinet acknowledged the order of an additional 10 million doses of the Pfizer (PFE.N)/BioNTech (22UAy.DE) COVID-19 vaccine, and assigned Ministry of Public Health’s Department of Disease Control to sign the deal with Pfizer (Thailand) Co., Ltd. and BioNTech SE. This will sum the purchase of the mRNAvaccines to 30 million doses, which will be delivered in the 4thquarter of 2021.
The Prime Minister thanked all concerned agencies for acquiring the 30 million doses of Pfizer vaccines, as well as other types of COVID-19 vaccine, for the people, and called for clear and proper vaccine distribution plan and publicity.
With this deal, almost all types of vaccines will be available in the country, be it, mRNA (Pfizer and Moderna), inactivated whole virus particle vaccine (Sinovac and Sinopharm), and viral vector vaccine (AstraZeneca). Pfizer vaccine has been conditionally approved by Thailand’s Food and Drug Administration on June 24, 2021, and is registered for those who are 12 years of age an above.

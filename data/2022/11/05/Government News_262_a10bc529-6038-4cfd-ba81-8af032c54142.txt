PM emphasizes importance of open data for transparency of public administration
March 5, 2022, at 1330hrs, at Pullman King Power Hotel Bangkok, Prime Minister and Defense Minister Gen. Prayut Chan-o-cha delivered a keynote address via a videoconference on occasion of the International Open Data Day 2022. Government Spokesperson Thanakorn Wangboonkongchana disclosed gist of the meeting as follows:
The Prime Minister expressed pleasure to be part of the International Open Data Day 2022. Realizing the cruciality of technology advancement and an increase of data information, the Thai Government has laid the foundation for Big Data development and data integration system to ensure access of all sectors to data, and utilization of the data for tangible development in various dimensions. This also encourages public participation to crosscheck the Government’s administration to ensure transparency. The Open Data Hackathon 2022 is also launched today to promote data management capacity enhancement and exchange of knowledge on the public sector’s open data.
The Prime Minister was confident that the event would foster collaboration in the development of an updated, connected and transparent open database, leading to the digitalization of public administration for national stability, prosperity, and sustainability.
The International Open Data Day 2022 is organized by Digital Government Development Agency (Public Organization) under the theme: “Unlock data to Innovation for A better Life” with an aim to present the benefits of open public data disclosure for public welfare and safety.

​Public warned not to buy COVID-19 medicine without prescription
August 4, 2022, Government Spokesperson Thanakorn Wangboonkongchana disclosed that Prime Minister and Defense Minister Gen. Prayut Chan-o-cha made a warning to the public not to buy Covid-19 drug without medical consultation/prescription, as it can be dangerous to their health. The Department of Health was reported on the online sales of Molnupiravir, and notified the police for further investigation and prosecution to ensure public safety. The Prime Minister affirmed sufficiency of COVID-19 medicines and medical supplies in Thai public health system, and called for the people to comply with the instruction and advice of Ministry of Public Health. COVID-19 medicines should only be taken as necessary upon the doctor’s judgment. Not all the infected patients will be prescribed with the medicine. Those who found themselves infected may call the Hotline 1330 to inform their symptoms for medical assessment. If they need medication, Covid-19 drug will be sent directly to their home.
As of today (August 4, 2022), a total of 2,166 new confirmed cases is reported, all of which were found domestically. The total of 29 deaths and 2,700 recoveries were reported with 21,377 active cases. From January 1, 2022, cumulative number of cases are 2,375,290, and cumulative number of recoveries, 2,377,362. There are 905 active case patients who have developed pneumonia.

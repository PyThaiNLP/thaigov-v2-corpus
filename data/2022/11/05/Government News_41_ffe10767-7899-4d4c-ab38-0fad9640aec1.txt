Concerned agencies join forces in giving COVID-19 vaccines to vulnerability groups
July 26, 2021, Government Spokesperson Anucha Burapachaisri disclosed that Prime Minister and Defense Minister Gen. Prayut Chan-o-cha has made an order for all concerned agencies to work in an integrative manner to ensure that the people, especially those in the vulnerability groups, the elderlies, those with 7 underlying non-communicable diseases, and pregnant women, have had fair access to the COVID-19 vaccines.
According to the Government Spokesperson, Ministry of Social Development and Human Security’s Department of Older Persons, social development and human security volunteers, Bangkok Metropolitan Administration’s Health Department, district offices, military, police, and public health volunteers have formed an integrative force to vaccinate the elderlies, those with 7 underlying non-communicable diseases, and pregnant women in communities throughout Bangkok, and gave them preventive advices, including Ministry of Public Health’s D-M-H-T-T measure, in a bid to reduce risk.
As of July 25, 2021, cumulative number of COVID-19 vaccination in Thailand totals 15,994,842 doses (1st shot: 12,339,985, and 2nd shot: 3,654,857), out of which 5,318,434 doses (1st shot: 3,341,846, and 2nd shot: 988,294) were given in Bangkok. The Prime Minister also thanked all concerned sectors and agencies for their cooperation and sacrifice in helping out the Thai people against the COVID-19 crisis.

​PM chairs auspicious ceremony of 5 religions to commemorate Her Majesty Queen Sirikit the Queen Mother’s 90th birthday anniversary on August 12, 2022
The Government organized auspicious ceremony of 5 religions to commemorate Her Majesty Queen Sirikit the Queen Mother’s 90th birthday anniversary on August 12, 2022.
August 12, 2022, at 0815hrs, at Bhakdi Bodin Building, Government House, Prime Minister Gen. Prayut Chan-o-cha, together with Mrs. Naraporn Chan-o-cha, spouse, presided over the auspicious ceremony of 5 religions to commemorate Her Majesty Queen Sirikit the Queen Mother’s 90th birthday anniversary on August 12, 2022. Participating in the ceremony were religious leaders and representatives, Speaker of the House, President of the Senate, President of the Supreme Court, presidents of organizations under the Constitution and spouses, members of the cabinet and spouses, Prime Minister’s Secretary-General, Bangkok Governor, heads of military and police, heads of government agencies, Bangkok permanent secretary, and the people sector.
Upon arrival, the Prime Minister paid homage to Her Majesty Queen Sirikit the Queen Mother’s portrait by offering traditional cone-shaped floral receptacles. Auspicious ceremony of the 5 religions, then, began with the Buddhist rite chanting ceremony by 10 Buddhist monks, followed by the Du'a prayer (Islamism); prayers from Catholic Bishops' Conference of Thailand (Roman Catholicism); Brahmin prayers (Hinduism), and the Ardas prayer (Sikhism).
The Prime Minister, then, presented souvenirs to religious leaders and representatives, paid homage to the Tri-Ratna, and bowed before Her Majesty Queen Sirikit the Queen Mother’s portrait to end the ceremony.

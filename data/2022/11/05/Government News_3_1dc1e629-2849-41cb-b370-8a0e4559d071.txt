Govt fares well on narcotics suppression
Govt fares well on narcotics suppression
Deputy Government Spokesperson Ratchada Thanadirek disclosed that Thailand has recently managed to suppress and arrest many cases of narcotics networks, which demonstrates efficient collaboration between Office of the Narcotics Control Board (ONCB), Internal Security Operations Command (ISOC), Armed Force Security Center, the Royal Thai Police’s Bureau of Narcotics, the Customs Department, people sector, and foreign agencies. As the number of cases using Thailand as transit point for trafficking of narcotics has continuously increased since 2019, the Thai Government, through ONCB, has liaised with other 5 Mekong countries, namely, China, Myanmar, Lao PDR, Cambodia, and Vietnam, as well as UNODC, to organize ameeting on July 5, 2021 to discuss about proper solutions to the problem.
According to the statistics on drug-related arrest in Thailand during October 2020-June 2021, 344 million pills of Methamphetamine, 20,662kg of ICE, 2,760kg of Heroin, and 279,868 pills of Ecstasy have been confiscated. Of all the arrests, 84 cases were attempted trafficking of narcotics out of the country (39,002 pills of Methamphetamine, 72.08kg of ICE, 285.69kg of Heroin, and 1,922 pills of Ecstasy).
Ministry of Justice has also undertaken pro-active approaches to seize and forfeit assets from those involved in narcotics crimes.Assets forfeited during the first quarter of 2021 worth 4,549 million Baht, an increase of 3,480 million Baht from the same period last year. Ministry of Justice has set the target of 6 billion Baht for asset forfeiture.
According to the Deputy Government Spokesperson, Prime Minister and Defense Minister Gen. Prayut Chan-o-cha has placed high importance on narcotics suppression, which has been set as a national agenda. Three related laws are also under revision, i.e., draft Act on Enactment of Narcotics Code, draft Narcotics Code, and draft Act on Narcotics-Related Legal Cases.

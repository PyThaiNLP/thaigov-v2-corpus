Ministry of Justice continues to hold “Household Debt Settlement Mediation” in every province until August 15
June 28, 2022, Government Spokesperson Thanakorn Wangboonkongchana disclosed that Prime Minister and Defense Minister Gen. Prayut Chan-o-cha acknowledged and was pleased with the effort of Ministry of Justice, led by Minister Somsak Thepsutin, to address household debts through the implementation of the “Household Debt Settlement Mediation” program, which was organized 39 times in different provinces throughout the country, and managed to help 28,314 people settle debts of over 6.602 billion Baht (99.42% of debtors who took part in the mediation). This is considered a success of the Government’s policy which designated the year 2022 as “the year to solve household debt problem”.
According to the Government Spokesperson, Ministry of Justice was also assigned to work together with the Student Loan Fund and 16 financial institutions to organize household debt settlement mediation across the country. Debt mediation participants would initially receive such privileges as: 1) extension of debt repayment; 2) penalty reduction / reduction of interests /reduction of monthly installment rate; 3) will not be legally prosecuted; 4) no asset confiscation / auction; and any other privileges. Debtors are encouraged to take part in the “Household Debt Settlement Mediation” program and “Ministry of Justice Meet the People (4 Regions)”, which is held under the collaboration of the Legal Executive Department, and the Rights and Liberties Protection Department. In June 2022 alone, the event has been organized in 20 provinces, namely, Krabi, Yala, Narathiwat, Nong Bua Lamphu, Udon Thani, Ratchaburi, Uttaradit, Phrae, Phetchabun, Tak, Lamphun, Phayao, Chiang Rai, Uthai Thani, Ayutthaya, Mae Hongson, Nan, Nakhon Sawan, Phichit, and Kamphaeng Phet.
The “Household Debt Settlement Mediation” program and “Ministry of Justice Meet the People (4 Regions)” will continue to be held until August 15, 2022 in a bid to rush in helping out the people in accordance with the Prime Minister’s order. Those who are interested may inquire for further information at the Legal Executive Department’s HOTLINE 1111 or 02-881-4999; or the Rights and Liberties Protection Department at 0 2141 2768-73, or Legal Executive Department’s Dispute Resolution Service Center at 02 881 4840.

PM orders immediate hospitalization of COVID-19 patients under 608 groups who are classified yellow
April 5, 2022, Government Spokesperson Thanakorn Wangboonkongchana disclosed that Prime Minister and Defense Minister Gen. Prayut Chan-o-cha expressed concern over COVID-19 infected patients under the 608 group, which comprises the elderlies (60 years and over), people with chronic diseases, pregnant women, and those with the weight of over 90 kg., and ordered concerned agencies to accelerate screening of patients under the 608 group, and provide immediate treatment to those who are classified yellow. In this case, they may be hospitalized at either public or private hospitals free of charge under UCEP Plus. However, if there are limited number of beds, concerned agencies are instructed to liaise with local networks to put the patients under medical care the soonest. People under the 608 group, including small children, are also urged to receive vaccine, as well as the booster dose, to reduce risk of criticality and death.
As of today (April 5, 2022), a total of 21,088 new confirmed cases is reported, among which 20,995 were found domestically, and 93 from overseas traveling. The total of 91 deaths and 27,519 recoveries were reported with 250,145 active cases. From January 1, 2022, cumulative number of cases are 1,534,140, and cumulative number of recoveries, 1,313,333. There are 1,892 patients who have developed pneumonia (an average of 24 persons per province).

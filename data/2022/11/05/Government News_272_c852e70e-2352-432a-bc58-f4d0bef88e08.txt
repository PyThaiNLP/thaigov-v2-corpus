Govt seeks both urgent, short and long-term solutions to energy price hike to mitigate people’s plight
March 15, 2022, at 1430hrs, at the Green Room, Thai Khu Fah Building, Government House, Prime Minister and Defense Minister Gen. Prayut Chan-o-cha presided over a meeting to discuss the energy issue and people’s plight mitigation. Government Spokesperson Thanakorn Wangboonkongchana disclosed gist of the meeting as follows:
At the meeting, the Prime Minister ordered all concerned agencies, i.e., Ministry of Energy, Ministry of Finance, Ministry of Labor, Ministry of Commerce, Ministry of Agriculture and Cooperatives, the Bank of Thailand, Office of National Economic and Social Development Council, and other concerned agencies, to come up with solutions and mitigation guidelines for the people, especially those who have been affected by energy price hike, low-income earners, workers, etc.
The Prime Minister has handed down a policy to implement proper measures that have the least impact on the people to solve the problem within a limited budget. There should be both urgent, short, and long-term measures for sustainable solutions.

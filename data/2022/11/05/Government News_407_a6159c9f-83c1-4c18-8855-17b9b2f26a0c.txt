​The PM recommended adapting faster treatment to reduce the number of critically ill patients, including the need to speed up more vaccination.
July 16, 2022, Government Spokesperson Thanakorn Wangboonkongchana revealed that Prime Minister and Minister of Defense, Gen. Prayut Chan-o-cha suggested the Ministry of Health to adapt faster treatment system in order to reduce patients as believed that the COVID-19 outbreak within the country might be higher after August 12.  However, the COVID-19 alert-level system is still believed to be level 2 green because Thai people are familiar with how to prevent and protect themselves after experiencing COVID-19 over the past 2 years, leading Thai people to still maintain relatively suitable personal protection measures, as well as standards of public transport, remains concentrated.
As of today (July 16, 2022), 2,025 new cases of COVID-19 were reported, with 2,023 domestic cases, 2 foreign cases, and 20 fatalities. 23,867 people have received treatment, and 2,181 recovered patients have gone home. Since January 1, 2022, with 2,333,566 confirmed patients, 2,333,662 people have recovered from diseases, and 785 pneumonia patients have been admitted to hospitals.

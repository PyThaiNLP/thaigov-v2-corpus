Govt Spokesperson: Amendment of Doping Control Act in Sports to complete in January 2022
Government Spokesperson Thanakorn Wangboonkongchanadisclosed that Prime Minister and Defense Minister Gen. Prayut Chan-o-cha ordered an immediate action to address the issue which the World Anti-Doping Agency (WADA) ruled that Thailand is non-compliant to the 2021 Anti-Doping Code, as a result of which the national flag is not allowed to be flown at regional, continental or world championships, or at other major events. Amendment of 24 sections of the Doping Control Act in Sports B.E. 2555, related to WADA standards, will be submitted to and scrutinized by the cabinet, in its meeting on December 28. The amendment process is expected to complete by January 2022.
Upon the completion of the amendment process, concerned agency will liaise with WADA on the lift of ban. Thailand will also make an appeal to the Court of Arbitration for Sport (CAS)again on the use of national flag at the international sports events.
According to the Government Spokesperson, the Government does not stay idle on the matter and strives to address the problem from the very start to reach satisfactory solution the soonest. This is in a bid to prevent socio-economic impact and to boost morale of the athletes and concerned staffs.

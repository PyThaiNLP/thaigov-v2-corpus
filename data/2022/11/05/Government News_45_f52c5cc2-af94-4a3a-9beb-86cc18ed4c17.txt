PM appreciated the U.S. Government’s hospitality for 1.54 million doses of Pfizer vaccines.
1.54 million doses of Pfizer vaccines arrived in Thailand as the Prime Minister expressed appreciation for the United States Government’s Hospitality and reiterated the doses have to administer only to frontline medical workers and targeted groups of people, not to VIPs.
July 30, 2021, 4 a.m.,Deputy Government Spokesperson Traisuree Taisaranakul disclosed today that Deputy Prime Minister and Minister of Public Health Anutin Charnvirakul and senior health officials represented the Thai Government receiving the delivery of 1.54 million doses of Pfizer vaccines donated by the United States at Suvarnabhumi Airport.
The shipment arrived at Suvarnabhumi Airport at 04.30 a.m. on flight 3S530 of AeroLogic airlines, after which they must be stored in a refrigerator at -70 to -90 degrees Celsius, before distributing.
Prime Minister and Defense Minister Gen. Prayut Chan-o-cha expressed appreciation for the U.S. Government’s hospitality which U.S. Government always have for Thailand, especially when Thailand is facing the COVID-19 pandemic. The Prime Minister also emphasized that the vaccine distribution must follow the plan in which the doses would be administered only to frontline medical workers and target groups, not to VIPs, Ms. Traisuree said.
For the target group on a proposed distribution of 1.54 million doses of Pfizer vaccines will be received, consisting of:
1) Frontline medical personnel working on a COVID-19 mission (as the 3rd booster dose) 700,000 doses 2) People at high risk of dying from COVID-19 infection with Thai nationality 645,000 doses 3) Elderly people, people with chronic diseases in 7 disease groups, pregnant women with the gestational age of 12 weeks or more, foreigners living in Thailand (focus on the elderly and chronic disease, pregnant women with a gestational age of 12 weeks or more, foreign travelers who need to receive the Pfizer vaccine such as diplomats, students) 150,000 doses 4) Conduct a research study. (Approved by the Ethics Research Committee) 5,000 doses and 5) Reserved for countering the outbreak 40,000 doses.

PM to visit Southern border provinces on Dec 15
December 14, 2021, Government Spokesperson Thanakorn Wangboonkong chana disclosed that Prime Minister and Defense Minister Gen. Prayut Chan-o-cha, together with concerned ministers, is scheduled to travel to Yala and Pattani provinces on December 15, 2021 to follow up on the implementation progress of the Government’s policies on socio-economic and security development in the Southern border provinces. His schedule and missions are as follows:
In the morning, the Prime Minister and delegation will attend a meeting at Yala Provincial Government Center Building to follow up on the implementation progress of the Government’s policies on socio-economic and security development in the Southern border provinces. Focus will be made on uplifting people’s quality of life, socio-economic development under the “Triangle of Stability, Prosperity and Sustainability” model project, border security management, COVID-19 situation administration, preparation on easing of area-based control measures, and operation progress of the strategic committee for development of Southern border provinces.
The Prime Minister will, then, chair the launching of “Poverty Eradication and Sustainable Development of People of All Ages According to Sufficiency Economy Philosophy in Southern Border Provinces” operation under the “One SBPAC official, One Poor Household” framework. He will also chair the meeting on COVID-19 situation administration and development of the Southern border provinces.
In the afternoon, the Prime Minister is scheduled to visit black crab (scylla serrata) farming community in Pattani’s Yaring district. Black crabs will be promoted as new alternative economic animal, and the district will be developed as the world’s black crab city under the grassroots economic development framework, to be implemented by the Southern Border Provinces Administrative Centre (SBPAC), with the collaboration of Prince of Songkla University (Pattani Campus), the Fishery Department, Department of Marine and Coastal Resources, and local farmer community enterprises. This is in accordance with the Prime Minister’s policy to promote employment and job diversity to increase revenues of the Southern border farmers, and revive natural resources and environment in a tangible manner.

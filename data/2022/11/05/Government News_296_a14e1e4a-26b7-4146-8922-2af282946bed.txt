PM emphasizes strict traffic law enforcement during Songkran
April 12, 2022, Government Spokesperson Thanakorn Wangboonkongchana disclosed that Prime Minister and Defense Minister Gen. Prayut Chan-o-cha expressed concern over public traveling during Songkran Holiday. It is expected that the highest number of people will commute in and out of town on April 12. Concerned authorities and agencies are instructed to tighten traffic law enforcement, and periodically open special lanes to alleviate traffic congestion. Drivers of public vehicles must undergo alcohol and drug test to ensure their driving capacity, and passenger services must be well maintained through proper rotation of drivers.
The Prime Minister also thanked and gave moral support to all concerned officials and staffs who have to work to ensure commuters’ safety during Songkran Holiday, and urge everyone to join forces in preventing the spread of COVID-19 through observing maximum universal prevention. Songkran activities must be held strictly in accordance with the COVID free setting. Ministry of Public Health has been assigned to assess post-Songkran COVID-19 situation in hope that the number of patients remains under control.
As of today (April 12, 2022), a total of 19,982 new confirmed cases is reported, among which 19,930 were found domestically, and 52 from overseas traveling. The total of 101 deaths and 28,057 recoveries were reported with 237,399 active cases. From January 1, 2022, cumulative number of cases are 1,702,419, and cumulative number of recoveries, 1,493,672.There are 2,056 active case patients who have developed pneumonia (an average of 27 persons per province). Bed occupancy rate is 30%. Cumulative number of vaccinations across the country is 131,509,798 doses (1st shot: 55,955,320, 2ndshot: 50,617,229, 3rd shot 22,577,925, and 4th shot: 2,359,324.

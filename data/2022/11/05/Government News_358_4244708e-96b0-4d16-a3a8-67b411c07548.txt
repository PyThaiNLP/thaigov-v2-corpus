PM pleased with declining number of COVID-19 confirmed cases
June 14, 2022, Government Spokesperson Thanakorn Wangboonkongchana disclosed that Prime Minister and Defense Minister Gen. Prayut Chan-o-cha is pleased with the improving COVID-19 situation, which saw the number of new confirmed cases falling below 2,000 for 2 days in a role. However, he emphasized that it is still important for everyone to keep their guard high and comply with public health measures to prevent recurrence of the disease spread. The Prime Minister also instructed Ministry of Public Health to continue to closely monitor the situation, and educate the public on vaccination and Long COVID to boost their confidence in coping with the disease.
As of today (June 14, 2022), a total of 1,833 new confirmed cases is reported, among which 1,832 were found domestically, and 1 from overseas traveling. The total of 19 deaths and 2,155 recoveries were reported with 21,445 active cases. From January 1, 2022, cumulative number of cases are 2,265,062, and cumulative number of recoveries, 2,268,190. There are 656 active case patients who have developed pneumonia. Cumulative number of vaccinations across the country from February 28, 2021 to June 13, 2022 is 138,441,640 doses (1st shot: 56,826,807, 2nd shot: 52,861,813, 3rd shot: 25,093,138, and 4th shot: 3,659,882.

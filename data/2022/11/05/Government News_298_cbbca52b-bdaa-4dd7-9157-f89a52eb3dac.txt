PM orders Ministry of Public Health and other concerned agencies to prepare for possible post-Songkran COVID-19 clusters
April 16, 2022, Government Spokesperson Thanakorn Wangboonkongchana disclosed that Prime Minister and Defense Minister Gen. Prayut Chan-o-cha expressed appreciation to all concerned authorities, be it, police, military, and civilians, for taking good care and ensuring safety of the people, and instructed them to be prepared for the return of commuters toward the end of Songkran Holiday. He also thanked business owners and the public for complying with COVID-19 preventive measures during the holiday.
Ministry of Public Health and other concerned agencies are ordered to prepare for possible post-Songkran COVID-19 clusters. National Health Security Office (NHSO) has already had additional COVID-19 hotlines put in place. Meanwhile, the public are urged to monitor their health for 7-10 days after returning from Songkran Holiday. Should certain symptoms, such as fever, running nose, or coughing, develop, they must immediately seek medical advice, and undergo the test.
As of today (April 16, 2022), a total of 18,892 new confirmed cases is reported, among which 18,810 were found domestically, and 82 from overseas traveling. The total of 125 deaths and 22,220 recoveries were reported with 221,452 active cases. From January 1, 2022, cumulative number of cases are 1,788,749, and cumulative number of recoveries, 1,595,484. There are 2,062 active case patients who have developed pneumonia (an average of 27 persons per province). Bed occupancy rate is 26.7%.

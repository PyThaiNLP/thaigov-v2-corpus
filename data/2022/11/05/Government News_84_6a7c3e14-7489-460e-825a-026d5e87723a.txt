Govt Spokesperson: PM thanks for smoothly-run censure debate
September 4, 2021, at 1030hrs, at Sappaya-Sapasathan (Parliament House), Government Spokesperson Thanakorn Wangboonkongchana disclosed that Prime Minister and Defense Minister Gen. Prayut Chan-o-cha thanked for all the votes he received from the MPs, both in favor and against, and for the uneventful censure debate. This was also a good opportunity for the Government to clarify several issues of current interest, as its important duties for the time being are to safeguard people's health and wellbeing, and to implement economic rehabilitation schemes. He also confirmed the coalition parties' strength and joint responsibility to tackle the COVID-19 situation in a bid to lead the country out of the crisis for the people's life to return to normalcy.
Results of the censure debate voting are as follows:
1)    Prime Minister and Defense Minister Gen. Prayut Chan-o-cha: 264 votes in favor, 208 against, and 3 abstentions
2)    Deputy Prime Minister and Public Health Minister Anutin Charnvirakul: 269 votes in favor, 196 against, and 11 abstentions
3)    Labor Minister Suchart Chomklin: 263 votes in favor, 201 against, and 10 abstentions
4)    Transport Minister Saksayam Chidchob: 269 votes in favor, 195 against, and 10 abstentions
5)    Agriculture and Cooperatives Minister Chalermchai Sri-on: 270 votes in favor, 199 against, and 8 abstentions
6)    Digital Economy and Society Minister Chaiwut Thanakamanusorn: 267 votes in favor, 202 against, and 9 abstentions
The Prime Minister and five ministers won a vote of confidence from the House.

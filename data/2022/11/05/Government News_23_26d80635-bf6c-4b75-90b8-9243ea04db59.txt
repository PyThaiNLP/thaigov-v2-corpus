Thailand and Myanmar to increase border cooperation
July 5, 2021, at 1130hrs, at the Ivory Room, Thai Khu Fah Building, Government House, H.E. U Myo Myint Than, Ambassador of Republic of the Union of Myanmar to Thailand, paid a courtesy call on Prime Minister and Defense Minister Gen. Prayut Chan-o-cha on occasion of his completion of tenure. Government Spokesperson Anucha Burapachaisri disclosed gist of the meeting as follows:
The Prime Minister welcomed and thanked the Myanmar Ambassador for his active role in promoting relations between Thailand and Myanmar throughout the 4 years of his tenure. As each other’s neighboring country and members of ASEAN family, the Prime Minister sincerely hopes that unrest situation in Myanmar would be solved soon through the peaceful means.
The Myanmar Ambassador expressed appreciation toward the Prime Minister for the meeting, and to concerned agencies for their support throughout his tenure. He hopes for Thailand’s continued support to the incoming Myanmar Ambassador, andfor continuous relations and cooperation.
Both parties exchanged views and agreed on an increase of cooperation in addressing border problems, i.e., illegal smuggling, and the issue of remaining Myanmar workers in Thailand, among others. The Prime Minister thanked Myanmar workers for their contribution to Thailand’s development. Once the COVID-19 situation eases, they are welcomed back to work in the country.
With regard to trade cooperation, the Prime Minister called on Myanmar to review the decision on import suspension of more than 50 consumer goods through land border crossings, which has significantly affected both Thai exporters and Myanmar consumers, as well as total trade values between the two countries.

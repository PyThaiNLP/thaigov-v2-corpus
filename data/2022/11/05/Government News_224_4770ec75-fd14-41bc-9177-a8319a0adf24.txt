​PM pleased with the tourism news after Krabi was named Thailand’s Most Welcoming City, emphasized the readiness of resuming the Test and Go Program
January 29, 2022, Ms. Traisuree Taisaranakul, Deputy Government Spokesperson, disclosed that the Prime Minister was very pleased with the success of Thailand’s tourism industry after Krabi was named Thailand’s Most Welcoming City and received the award from Traveller Review Awards 2022, held by the Booking.com, a leading digital travel platform. The other cities in Thailand on the “most welcoming” list were Trat, Chiang Rai, Chiang Mai, and Nakhon Ratchasima.
Deputy Government Spokesperson revealed that the success came from the government’s policy to reopen the country and believed that the award will help boost more tourists to visit Thailand. Krabi was praised as the major tourist destination from domestic and international travellers, while the campaign “Krabi Even More Amazing” also received a lot of tourists’ attention to visit the province as well as the other pilot cities that reopen for the tourists.
According to the Deputy Government Spokesperson, the Prime Minister appreciated that the related agencies in the tourism industry such as tourism companies, government agencies, and local people presented their warm hospitality and being a good host. The Prime Minister also stated that Thailand is ready to resume a new application for the Test and Go Program, starting February 1, 2022. The program will be the opportunity for the tourism industry and the economy of Thailand to revive from the impact of the pandemic.

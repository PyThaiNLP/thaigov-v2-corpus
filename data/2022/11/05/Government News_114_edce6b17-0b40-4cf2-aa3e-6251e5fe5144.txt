PM: No plan for House dissolution
PM: No plan for House dissolution
October 12, 2021, at 1400hrs, following the weekly cabinet meeting, Government Spokesperson ThanakornWangboonkongchana disclosed Prime Minister and Defense Minister Gen. Prayut Chan-o-cha’s response to the press on various matters.
The Prime Minister clarified about the country reopening that it is now at the preparation process. Nevertheless, he urged everyone not to drop their guard, and remain compliant with COVID-19 measures to prevent possible disease recurrence. Center for COVID-19 Situation Administration (CCSA) will meet on October 14 to discuss further on the detail of the country reopening.
With regard to an issue of monk’s inappropriate behavior found at fault by the Sangha Supreme Council, the Prime Minister stated that this matter will be handled by National Office of Buddhism in accordance with the Sangha Supreme Council’s ruling.
The Prime Minister also affirmed that there is no talk on the House dissolution nor the reelection. There will be only local elections. He has neither heard about a nomination of anotherprime ministerial candidate by Palang Pracharath Party, andcalled on the media not to publish news and information that could cause public confusion.
On the current flood situation, the Prime Minister affirmed the Government’s commitment to promptly provide relief to flood-affected victims in all areas in accordance with existing rule and regulation. Concerned agencies have been ordered to rush in surveying damages and to request for budget allocation accordingly.

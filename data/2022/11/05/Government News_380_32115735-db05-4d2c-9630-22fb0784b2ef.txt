PM is confident that Thailand is ready to welcome all tourists with an exemption from the 'Thailand Pass' starting on July 1
On June 29, 2022, The Government Spokesperson Thanakorn Wangboonkongchana disclosed that Prime Minister and Minister of Defense, Gen. Prayut Chan-o-cha, expressed his confidence that all Thai agencies are prepared to welcome tourists.  As The Centre for Covid-19 Situation Administration (CCSA) has agreed to relax preventive measures and control diseases for entering the country by approved the removal of the Thailand Pass registration scheme or CoE for foreigners, effective 1 July 2022. Foreign nationals are only required to show proof of either a certificate of vaccination or the results of the COVID test if have not been fully vaccinated.  Besides, the Prime Minister also reminded Ministry of Public Health, Ministry of Tourism and Sports, and related agencies to prepare systems and staffs in the area appropriately, as tourism is a major source of income for the country’s economy.  However, the Prime Minister confirmed that there is no fear of a re-emergence of the COVID-19 outbreak because Thailand underlines safe tourism, and the most fundamental thing is to adhere the universal prevention measures, for example, social distancing, washing hands, and still recommending masks to be always worn.
As of today, there have been 2,569 new cases of COVID-19 reported, with 2,569 domestic cases and 0 foreign cases, as well as 14 fatalities. 23,029 people have received treatment, and 1,984 recovered patients have gone home. Since January 1, there are 2,296,785 confirmed patients, 2,298,063 people have recovered from diseases, and 665 pneumonia patients have been admitted to hospitals.

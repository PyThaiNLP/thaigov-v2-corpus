​PM accepts 415,040 doses of AstraZeneca vaccines from UK
August 2, 2021, at 09.30 hrs, at the Purple Room, Thai Khu Fah Building, Government House, Prime Minister and Defense Minister Gen. Prayut Chan-o-cha presided over the handover ceremony of COVID-19 vaccines given by the Government of the United Kingdom. Attending the ceremony was H.E. Mr. Evan Jones, Chargé D'affaires a.i., UK Embassy in Thailand, as the representative of the UK Government. Government Spokesperson Anucha Burapachaisri disclosed gist of the meeting as follows:
The Prime Minister first conveyed felicitations toward Her Majesty Queen Elizabeth II on behalf of the Thai Government and people, and his regards to UK Prime Minister Boris Johnson. He, then, thanked the UK Government for granting 415,040 doses of AstraZeneca vaccines to Thailand which reflects over 400 years of long-standing and tightknit relations and friendship between the two countries. The vaccines donated by the UK Government would help Thailand and its economy to move forward in a strong and stable manner. The Prime Minister commended UK for being a role model for effective vaccine management and distribution, and expressed confidence that the two countries would soon overcome this challenge together.
The Chargé D'affaires a.i. of the UK Embassy in Thailand conveyed his felicitations to His Majesty King Rama X and members of the royal family, and expressed pleasure to be able to assist Thailand in this time of crisis. The 415,040 doses of AstraZeneca vaccines will arrive in Thailand tomorrow as part of the UK Government’s intent to donate vaccines to the friends in need.

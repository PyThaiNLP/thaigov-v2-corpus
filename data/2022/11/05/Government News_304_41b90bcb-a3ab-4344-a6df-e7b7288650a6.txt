​PM urges schools to be well prepared for opening of new semester in May
April 21, 2022, Government Spokesperson Thanakorn Wangboonkongchana disclosed that Prime Minister and Defense Minister Gen. Prayut Chan-o-cha called on education institutions throughout the country to prepare for the opening of the new semester in May 2022 in a bid to ensure safety according to the public health measures. Learning could be either onsite, online, or hybrid, while buildings, teachers and related equipment should be prepared for all the learning modes. Ministry of Education has been instructed to assign each educational service areas to conduct close monitoring to prevent COVID-19 related problem and crisis after school reopening.
According to the Government Spokesperson, schools must abide by and comply with universal prevention with six principal measures (social distancing, mask wearing, hand washing, temperature testing, reducing crowdedness, and cleaning or DMHT-RC), six additional measures (self-care, using serving spoon, eating hot foods, tracking movement, checking oneself, and quarantine or SSET-CQ), and the seven intensive measures for educational institutions: 1) self-evaluating via Thai Stop COVID Plus (TSC)+ platform, and reporting to MOE COVID, 2) small bubbles of classroom activities, 3) providing clean and nutritious food, 4) ensuring standardized ventilation and waste management system, 5) preparing school isolation facility, 6) providing sealed routes for commuting students and staff, and 7) setting up school pass system. At least 85% of school personnel and students must be properly vaccinated. Parents should also warn their children to always wear mask, and avoid large group of gathering and sharing things with friends. Classroom seating must be distanced and air conditioner should not be turned on for over 2 consecutive hours.
As of today (April 21, 2022), a total of 21,931 new confirmed cases is reported, among which 21,816 were found domestically, and 115 from overseas traveling. The total of 129 deaths and 24,619 recoveries were reported with 188,926 active cases. From January 1, 2022, cumulative number of cases are 1,882,795, and cumulative number of recoveries, 1,721,418. There are 2,021 active case patients who have developed pneumonia (an average of 26 persons per province). Bed occupancy rate is 25.1%.

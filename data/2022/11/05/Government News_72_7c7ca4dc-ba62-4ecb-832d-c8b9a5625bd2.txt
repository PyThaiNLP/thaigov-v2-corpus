Govt Spokesperson: SSO starts transferring subsidies to insured persons under Sections 33, 39, and 40
August 25, 2021, the newly-appointed Government Spokesperson Thanakorn Wangboonkongchana disclosed about the progress of subsidy transfer to employers and workers under Sections 33, 39, and 40 of the Social Security Act, B.E.2533 (1990) to alleviate the impact of the COVID-19 pandemic. On August 20, 2021, Ministry of Labor, through the Social Security Office of Thailand (SSO), has transferred the subsidy of 2,500 Baht/person to 249,235 Thai workers insured under Section 33 in 16 provinces (Nakhon Ratchasima, Rayong, Ratchaburi, Saraburi, Suphanburi, Kanchanaburi, Lopburi, Phetchabun, Prachuab Kirikhan, Prachinburi, Phetchaburi, Tak, Angthong, Nakhon Nayok, Samut Songkram, and Singburi), for the total amount of over 623 million Baht. As for the subsidies to be provided to employers in the above 16 provinces, at the amount according to the number of the employees (3,000 Baht/employee) but not exceeding 200 employees, SSO is in the process of verifying the information given by the employers in the E-Service system, and will transfer the subsidies to the given bank account of each employer every Friday.
According to the Government Spokesperson, SSO also started transferring subsidies to the bank account of 1.12 million insured persons under Section 39 in 13 provinces (Bangkok, Nakhon Pathom, Nonthaburi, Pathum Thani, Samut Prakarn, Samut Sakhon, Narathiwat, Pattani, Yala, Songkhla, Chachaoengsao, Chonburi, and Ayutthaya), for the total amount of 5.6 billion Baht. The transfer will also be made during August 24-26, 2021 to 4.5 million insured persons under Section 40 who reside in the above 13 provinces for the total amount of 22.482 billion Baht, and to 802,397 insured persons under Sections 39 and 40 in the 16 provinces (Nakhon Ratchasima, Rayong, Ratchaburi, Saraburi, Suphanburi, Kanchanaburi, Lopburi, Phetchabun, Prachuab Kirikhan, Prachinburi, Phetchaburi, Tak, Angthong, Nakhon Nayok, Samut Songkram, and Singburi) from August 27, 2021 onward.
The insured persons under Sections 33, 39, and 40 in the 13 lockdown provinces will receive the subsidies for 2 months, while those in the later-added lockdown 16 provinces will receive the subsidies for 1 month.
The Government Spokesperson added that Prime Minister and Defense Minister Gen. Prayut Chan-o-cha has much concern over the people of all groups and sectors, and instructed concerned agencies to take the best possible care of the people, especially SMEs entrepreneurs who have been heavily hit by the COVID-19 impact. The Government strives to alleviate the plights of Thai people in parallel with the effort to control COVID-19 situation.

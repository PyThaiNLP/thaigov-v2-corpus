PM: Thailand to acquire additional 65 million doses of COVID-19 vaccines for next year
November 16, 2021, at 1315hrs, Sofitel Krabi Phokeethra Golf & Spa Resort, Krabi, Prime Minister and Defense Minister Gen. Prayut Chan-o-cha stated about acquisition of the COVID-19 vaccine for next year that the contract has been made so far for the purchase of 65 million doses. In purchasing the COVID-19 vaccine, the Government will take into consideration R&D, and different technologies and forms of the vaccines, such as intranasal vaccines, to ensure that the Thai people receive the most suitable and effective COVID-19 vaccines.
Thailand has been on course in tackling the COVID-19 situation. The budget allocated for the remedies to alleviate economic impact of the COVID-19 on the Thai people accounts for 25% of GDP, the second largest in APEC after Chile. According to the Prime Minister, the Thai Government has genuinely concerned over people’s wellbeing, and strives to mitigate their plights and lead them to overcome the crisis.
The Prime Minister also expressed concern over the wellbeing of the farmers who have been impacted by the issues of current concern, e.g., household debts, pubic debts, gasoline oils, and instructed concerned ministers to look into the possibility of agricultural restructuring from upstream, midstream to downstream to reduce production cost, and farmland rental fees in accordance with the law.

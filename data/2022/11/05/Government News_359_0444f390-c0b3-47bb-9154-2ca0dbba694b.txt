Cabinet approves implementation of “BCG (U2T for BCG and Regional Development)” program
June 14, 2022, Government Spokesperson Thanakorn Wangboonkongchana disclosed a resolution of the cabinet meeting which approved for Ministry of Higher Education, Science and Innovation to implement “BCG (U2T for BCG and Regional Development)” program in 7,435 Tambons (sub-districts) of 77 provinces for the total budget of 3,566.28 million Baht.
The BCG (U2T for BCG and Regional Development) is aimed to enhance capacity and competitiveness of local BCG manufacturing and service sector with knowhow, technologies, and innovations. Target groups are new graduates of not over 5 years and local people/laid-off workers, whose basic skills related to the BCG economy will be enhanced. Thailand Community Big Data (TCD) will also be developed to cover every single area in the country.
Under the program more than 15,000 activities, e.g., technology transfer, business matching, marketing management, etc., will be held in 7,435 Tambons throughout the country. University responsible for respective Tambon will take charge in mobilizing the program, using TCD for planning and sustainable development of the community.
According to the Government Spokesperson, the “BCG (U2T for BCG and Regional Development)” program is expected to help increase sales of local products and services by no less than 10%, create employment of more than 68,350 new graduates, and generate over 600 million Baht/month.

​Deputy Prime Minister orders integrative operation from all concerned agencies to help flood-affected people
August 25, 2022, Deputy Secretary-General to the Prime Minister and Acting Government Spokesperson Anucha Burapachaisri disclosed that Deputy Prime Minister Gen. Prawit Wongsuwon expressed concern over possible flash flood and overflows which may affect people’s safety and wellbeing. According to Thailand Meteorological Department, strong monsoon trough lies across the North and the upper Northeast during August 25-26, 2022, while the southwest monsoon prevails over the Andaman Sea, Thailand and the Gulf of Thailand. With this, isolated heavy rain is forecasted in the North, the Northeast, the central including Bangkok and its vicinity, the East and the South (west coast) regions.
Pubic disaster prevention and mitigation agency also identified affected and risk-prone areas:
1. Areas at risk for flash flood, overflows, and inundation
North: The provinces of Tak (Mae Ramad and Tha Song Yang districts), Phitsanulok (Nakhon Thai, Noen Maprang, Chart Trakarn, and Wang Thong districts, and Phetchabun (Lon Kao, Lom Sak, and Khao Kho districts)
Central: The provinces of Nakhon Nayok (Muang district), Prachin Buri (Prajantakham, and Na Di districts), and Bangkok
2. Areas at risk for river overflow and inundation
Northeast:  The provinces of Roi Et (Sale Phum, Jung Harn, and Phon Thong districts), Maha Sarakham (Muang and Kantra Wichai districts), and Ubon Ratchathani (Muang district)
Central: The provinces of Ang Thong (Pa Mok and Wiset Chaicharn districts), and Phra Nakhon Si Ayutthaya (Sena, Phak Hai, Bang Ban, Bang Sai, Bang Pahan, and Phra Nakhon Si Ayutthaya districts)
3. Areas at risk for sea level rise at estuary and coastal lowland areas
Central: The provinces of Samut Sakhon, Samut Songkhram, Samut Prakarn, Nonthaburi, Chachoengsao, Chonburi, Rayong, Chanthaburi, Trat, Phetchaburi, Prachuab Kirikhan, and Bangkok
South: The provinces of Chumphon, Surat Thani, Nakhon Si Thammarat, Songkhla, Pattani, Narathiwat, Ranong, Phang-nga, Phuket, Krabi, Trang, and Satun
According to the acting Government Spokesperson, the Deputy Prime Minister has instructed Ministry of Defense to mobilize military troops and equipment, and integrate its operation with other concerned agencies to help out the people in need. Long-term water management must also be planned to store water for the dry season.

PM to have field trip in Songkhla and Phatthalung provinces
April 22, 2022, Government Spokesperson Thanakorn Wangboonkongchana disclosed that Prime Minister and Defense Minister Gen. Prayut Chan-o-cha is scheduled to take a field trip to Songkhla and Phathalung provinces on April 25, 2022 to follow up on the implementation progress of H.M. King Rama IX’s royal initiative project on flood prevention in Hat Yai district, and to hand down policies to chiefs and executives of local administrations. His itinerary is as follows:
In the morning, the Prime Minister will follow up and observe the implementation progress of H.M. King Rama IX’s royal initiative project on flood prevention in Hat Yai district through capacity enhancement of Phuminat Damri (Rama 1) Canal. Aside from flood mitigation in Hat Yai area, the canal area will also be used to contain water for use during the dry season. At present, 92.10% of the project (phase 2) has been completed, i.e., construction of the 2nd water gate at the mouth of the canal which enables control of water volume into the canal; and widening the canal from 24 meters to 70 meters. Construction of reinforced concrete wall is also 90% completed. At this stage, the canal could drain water during the rainy season at the speed of 1,000 cubic meters/second.
In the afternoon, the Prime Minister will chair the meeting to hand down policies to chiefs and executives of local administrations at the Provincial Hall, Phatthalung provinces. He, will, then, preside over the opening ceremony of an event to promote folk arts and culture, “Manorah”, in honor of Her Majesty Queen Sirikit the Queen Mother on occasion of her 90th birthday anniversary on August 12, 2022, before returning to Bangkok.

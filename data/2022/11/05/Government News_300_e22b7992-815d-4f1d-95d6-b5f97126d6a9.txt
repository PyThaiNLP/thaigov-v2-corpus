PM urges public to closely monitor health 7-10 days after Songkran Holiday
April 18, 2022, Government Spokesperson Thanakorn Wangboonkongchana disclosed that Prime Minister and Defense Minister Gen. Prayut Chan-o-cha urged the public and all other sectors to place great importance on post-Songkran 7-day period. Those who attended any Songkran activity or just returned from traveling, especially people who are under the 608 groups and who did not receive vaccines according to the set criteria, are strongly advised to monitor their health for 7-10 days, avoid meeting with others, always wear mask, and work from home, if possible. Should certain symptoms, such as fever, running nose, or coughing, develop, they must immediately seek medical advice, and undergo the test.
As of today (April 18, 2022), a total of 16,994 new confirmed cases is reported, among which 16,918 were found domestically, and 76 from overseas traveling. The total of 124 deaths and 25,910 recoveries were reported with 205,514 active cases. From January 1, 2022, cumulative number of cases are 1,823,518, and cumulative number of recoveries, 1,645,939.

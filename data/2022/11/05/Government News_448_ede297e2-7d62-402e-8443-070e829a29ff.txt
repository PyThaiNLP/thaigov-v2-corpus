Govt Spokesperson: Gen. Prayut respects Constitutional Court’s ruling
August 24, 2022, Deputy Secretary-General to the Prime Minister and Acting Government Spokesperson Anucha Burapachaisri disclosed about the ruling of the Constitutional Court to accept the petition on the 8-year tenure of the Prime Minister and agreed in a majority vote to suspend Gen. Prayut Chan-o-cha from official duties from August 24, 2022 onward until the Court has made its decision on the issue. According to the acting Government Spokesperson, Gen. Prayut Chan-o-cha will continue to perform his Minister of Defense duty, while Deputy Prime Minister Gen. Prawit Wongsuwon will assume his duty as acting Prime Minister. He affirmed that this change would not affect the Government’s national administration and public official duty performances, as well as implementation of various policies.
The acting Government Spokesperson added that Gen. Prayut Chan-o-cha called on the public to respect the ruling, and refrain from criticizing the Constitutional Court’s judgment for the country’s peacefulness and orderliness.

Cabinet approves amendment of Doping Control Act in Sports
December 28, 2021, Government Spokesperson Thanakorn Wangboonkongchana disclosed that the cabinet has made approval to the draft Royal Decree on Amendment of Doping Control Act in Sports (B.E. 2555), B.E. …, as proposed by Office of Council of State. The amendment is aimed to be in line with World Anti-Doping Agency (WADA)’s principles, and has been done as a royal decree to speed up the process. Upon the completion of the amendment process, Sports Authority of Thailand will liaise with WADA on the lift of ban including the use of national flag at the international sports events, especially at the Asian Games to be held in People’s Republic of China in 2022.
The amendment has been made on 24 sections of the Doping Control Act in Sports B.E. 2555, related to WADA standards, i.e., definition of terms, restructuring of related committees to ensure operating independence, and penalties.

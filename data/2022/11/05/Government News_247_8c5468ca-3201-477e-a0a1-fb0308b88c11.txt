PM concerned over widespread of fake news and information related to COVID-19
February 21, 2022, Government Spokesperson Thanakorn Wangboonkongchana disclosed that Prime Minister and Defense Minister Gen. Prayut Chan-o-cha expressed concerned over the fake and distorted information related to the COVID-19 pandemic which have been widely spread, especially via social media, and have caused misunderstanding, confusion, and panic to the public.
Ministry of Digital Economy and Society has been assigned to take immediate action in probing, and found a lot of fake news, including click baits, related to the COVID-19 situation. The Prime Minister, therefore, urged the public to make sure that the information they receive is legit before they share it. Provincial governors and provincial health offices are also instructed to manage the COVID-19 situation in their locality, and ensure proper treatment of infected patients according to the level of severity.
On February 21, 2022, a total of 18,883 new confirmed cases is reported, among which 18,721 were found domestically, and 162 from overseas traveling. The total of 32 deaths and 14,914 recoveries were reported with 166,397 active cases. From January 1, 2022, cumulative number of cases are 507,763, and cumulative number of recoveries, 373,651. Cumulative number of vaccinations across the country from February 28, 2021 to February 19, 2022 is 121,583,665 doses (1st shot: 53,100,356 persons, 2nd shot: 49,458,760 persons, and 3rd shot: 19,024,549 persons).

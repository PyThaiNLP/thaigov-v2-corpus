TPM: Public needs not worry as COVID-19 situation remains under control
PM: Public needs not worry as COVID-19 situation remains under control
July 5, 2022, Government Spokesperson Thanakorn Wangboonkongchana disclosed that Prime Minister and Defense Minister Gen. Prayut Chan-o-cha affirmed that the current COVID-19 situation remains under control even though higher number of newly-infected patients has been reported in certain areas. The public is called on to have confidence in the country’s public health system. There is no need for them to worry as long as they still keep their guard high and comply with public health measures, as well as receive full doses of vaccine. The Government and Center for COVID-19 Situation Administration (CCSA) are well prepared and stand ready to provide prompt support to Ministry of Public Health and other concerned agencies.
As of today (July 5, 2022), a total of 1,917 new confirmed cases is reported, among which 1,914 were found domestically, and 3 from overseas traveling. The total of 18 deaths and 2,282 recoveries were reported with 24,435 active cases. From January 1, 2022, cumulative number of cases are 2,310,582, and cumulative number of recoveries, 2,310,352. There are 705 active case patients who have developed pneumonia.

The Government extends Tax Incentives for E-Donation made to the Equitable Education Fund (EFF) to 2023, in order to promote equitable educational opportunities in the country
June 30, 2021, at the Government House, following the cabinet meeting on 29 June, 2021, Deputy Government Spokesperson Ratchada Thanadirek disclosed that the cabinet has approved the extension of tax incentives for individuals and registered ordinary partnerships who donate to the Equitable Education Fund (EFF) through the Revenue Department’s “E-Donation” from January 1, 2022 to December 31, 2023. The government further exempts Value Added Tax (VAT), Special Business Tax (SBT) and stamp duties for money incurred by the asset transfers, product sales, or execution of instruments regarding the fund.
The EFF, founded to expand more equitable educational opportunities by assisting the underprivileged students and improving teacher quality, has been funded by the government and the donors. In 2020, the fund directly helped 1.07 million students. In addition, donations to the EFF during 2019 to 2020 amount 23.71 million bath has been allocated to finance 46,331 students as follows:
(1) 40,167 students suffering from malnutrition, (2) 3,066 poor pupils both in and outside education system affected by Covid-19 in order for them to return to schools, (3) 1,298 students with poverty and disability to relieve the burden of their families and prevent them from dropping out during the pandemic, and (4) 1,800 students suffering from malnutrition who live in the remote areas to be provided adequate breakfast for a better learning ability throughout a year.

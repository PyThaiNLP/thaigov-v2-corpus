​PM advises parents to screen children’s health before going to school
May 17, 2022, Government Spokesperson Thanakorn Wangboonkongchana disclosed that Prime Minister and Defense Minister Gen. Prayut Chan-o-cha was pleased with the overall situation of the first day of school reopening and onsite teaching for the 1/2022 semester. Schools under Ministry of Education and Bangkok Metropolitan Administration, as well as private schools, have proved to be well prepared, both in terms of surveillance and control measures against the spread of COVID-19. Teachers, students, and school personnel all comply with the preventive measures through wearing hygienic mask, observing social distancing in the classrooms and corridors, regularly cleaning school areas, and staggering class hours and lunch break to vary the start and end times. Most of the students have also been vaccinated according to the advice of Ministry of Public Health.
The Prime Minister urged the parents to conduct screening of students’ health before allowing them to go to school to minimize the risk of the disease spread and infection. He also thanked the public and all concerned sectors, especially doctors and medical staffs, for their contribution to the continuously declining number of new COVID-19 cases, patients who have developed pneumonia, and death cases.
As of today (May 17, 2022), a total of 3,893 new confirmed cases is reported, among which 3,890 were found domestically, and 3 from overseas traveling. The total of 38 deaths and 7,323 recoveries were reported with 63,337 active cases. From January 1, 2022, cumulative number of cases are 2,159,542, and cumulative number of recoveries, 2,121,596. There are 1,245 active case patients who have developed pneumonia (an average of 16 persons per province). Bed occupancy rate is 16%. Cumulative number of vaccinations across the country from February 28, 2021 to May 15, 2022 is 135,671,274 doses (1st shot: 56,524,179, 2nd shot: 51,971,199, 3rd and 4th shot: 27,175,896.

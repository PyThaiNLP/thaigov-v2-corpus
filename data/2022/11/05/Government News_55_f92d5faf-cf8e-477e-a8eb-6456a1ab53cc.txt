PM expresses condolence to family of Swiss tourist who was found dead in Phuket
August 6, 2021, Government Spokesperson Anucha Burapachaisri disclosed that Prime Minister and Defense Minister Gen. Prayut Chan-o-cha was reported on the suspected murder of Mrs. Nicole Sauvain-Weisskopf, a Swiss citizen visiting Thailand under the Phuket Sandbox scheme, who was found dead in the area of Ton Ao Yon Waterfall, Wichit subdistrict, Mueang district, Phuket on August 5, 2021, and expressed condolences to the bereaved family. Both central and local police officers are now working together to expedite the investigation, and will constantly update the Prime Minister of the case.
Deputy Prime Minister and Minister of Foreign Affairs Don Pramudwinai has also made a direct phone call to the Ambassador of the Swiss Confederation to Thailand to convey his condolences to the bereaved family. Ministry of Foreign Affairs will collaborate with the Embassy of the Swiss Confederation in Thailand to update progress of the investigation.
According to the Government Spokesperson, the Prime Minister ordered concerned agencies to expedite the investigation to identify and arrest the culprit. He also instructed other Government agencies to strengthen their support to the tourists under Phuket Sandbox scheme, while Ministry of Foreign Affairs and Tourism Authority of Thailand have been assigned to liaise with other agencies to tighten safety and public health measures for Thai and foreign tourists. The Prime Minister also called for all the Thai people to help maintain and promote the country’s good image, especially among foreign tourists, in a bid for the country to generate more revenues and revitalize Thai economy.

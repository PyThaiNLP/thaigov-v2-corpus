PM: Bangkok governor election set by first half of 2022
December 13, 2021, at 1130hrs, at Santi Maitri Building, Government House, Prime Minister and Defense Minister Gen. Prayut Chan-o-cha responded to the query made by the press about the rally of Chana Rak Thin Network that the Government does not stay idle to the call of Chana Rak Thin Network. Deputy Prime Minister and Energy Minister Supattanapong Punmeechaow has been assigned to chair the fact-checking committee on the development of Chana special economic zone. Public hearing and SEA may have to be conducted to review the problem. The Prime Minister also emphasized the Government’s intent to uplift revenues and quality of life of the people in Southern border provinces. Solution to the conflict will be sought in accordance with the law. The issue will also be brought to the cabinet for further discussion.
The Prime Minister added that the election of the new Bangkok Governor is expected to take place no later than the first half of 2022. Since Bangkok is the country’s crucial area, he hoped that the people vote for a candidate with suitable qualification who is uncorrupted and could serve their needs. With regard to the reduction of penalties of offenders, the Prime Minister stated that such matter will be carefully reviewed in accordance with the laws and Ministry of Justice’s regulations.

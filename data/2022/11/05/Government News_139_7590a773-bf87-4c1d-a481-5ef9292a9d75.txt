PM and Thai delegation to attend UNFCCC COP26
Government Spokesperson Thanakorn Wangboonkongchana disclosed that Prime Minister and Defense Minister Gen. Prayut Chan-o-cha, together with the Thai delegation comprising Deputy Prime Minister and Minister of Foreign Affairs Don Pramudwinai, and Minister of Natural Resources and Environment Varawut Silpa-archa, is scheduled to attend the World Leaders Summit for the 26th United Nations Framework Convention on Climate Change Conference of the Parties (UNFCCC COP26) to be held in Glasgow, United Kingdom on November 1-2, 2021.
According to the Government Spokesperson, the Prime Minister will deliver a statement to demonstrate Thailand’s vision on the global major challenge, that is, the climate change. This will be an opportunity for the Prime Minister to present Thailand’s Long-Term Low Greenhouse Gas Emission Development Strategy (LT-LEDS), which is part of the country’s obligation under the Paris Agreement, and to highlight Thailand’s goal and active implementations through these times.
The Prime Minister will affirm Thailand’s commitment to join hands with other countries in realizing and setting policies to mitigate environmental issues that have been posing impact on the life and assets of everyone in the world. UK will host COP26 during October 31- November 12, 2021. National leaders from over 120 countries, and over 25,000 national representatives and high-level officials, have been invited to attend the meetings. This demonstrates commitment and collective action of national leaders to ensure constructive and concrete outcomes of COP26 against climate-related hazards.

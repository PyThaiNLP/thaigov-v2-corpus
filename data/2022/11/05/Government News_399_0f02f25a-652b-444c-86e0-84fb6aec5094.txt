PM pleased with increase of Thai labor quota for working in Israel’s agricultural sector
July 15, 2022, Government Spokesperson Thanakorn Wangboonkongchana disclosed that Prime Minister and Defense Minister Gen. Prayut Chan-o-cha was pleased with the sending of Thai labors to work in the agricultural sector of the State of Israel under “Thailand – Israel Cooperation on the Placement of Workers (TIC)” program, with the total of 3,759 workers having been sent in 2022. He also ordered Labor Minister Suchart Chomklin to instruct concerned agencies in facilitating, and enhancing the workers’ necessary skills in order to meet the market demand, and further increase employment opportunity of Thai labors in the global market.
According to the Government Spokesperson, Thai labor quota for working in the agricultural sector of the State of Israel in 2022 has increased by 1,800, from the year 2021, to 6,800 people, thanks to Thailand’s reputation in effectively administering the COVID-19 situation, and the reputation of Thai workers as being skillful and responsible.
The Prime Minister has always emphasized the importance of the labor sector as a driver for national development and revenue generation, and commended all concerned agencies for their integrative operation in capacity building of the Thai labors in all dimensions. He wished all the best for the workers who have been sent to Israel, and hoped they would bring back experiences gained from working there for the development of the nation and themselves.

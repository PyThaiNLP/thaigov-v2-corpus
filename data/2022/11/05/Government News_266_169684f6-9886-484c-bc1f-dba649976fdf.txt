Govt Spokesperson: Thailand’s neutrality on Russia-Ukraine situation is for best interest of nation and people
Government Spokesperson Thanakorn Wangboonkongchana clarified about the Government’s policy on Russia-Ukraine situation that Prime Minister and Defense Minister Gen. Prayut Chan-o-cha has handed down the policy since the beginning of the conflict to keep a balance at the international stage. Top priority must be to evacuate Thais in Ukraine, which has been successfully executed. Thailand’s position has always been to support dialogues, not violence, for peaceful resolution in accordance with the principles of the United Nations Charter or through a mutually agreed mechanism.
As ASEAN member, Thailand has also affirmed its position through the “ASEAN Foreign Ministers’ Statement on the Situation in Ukraine” to call on all relevant parties to exercise maximum restraint and make utmost efforts to pursue dialoguesthrough all channels, including diplomatic means to contain the situation, to de-escalate tensions, and to seek peaceful resolution in accordance with international law, the principles of the United Nations Charter and the Treaty of Amity and Cooperation in Southeast Asia.
The Government Spokesperson emphasized that the policy has been implemented for utmost interest of the nation and people. With the current challenge of COVID-19 pandemic, Thailand does not want to see any international conflict and tension, and strongly believes that resolution to the conflict can be achieve through the peaceful means.

PM orders concerned agencies to be on alert for possible flash flood and inundation during July 21-25
July 21, 2022, Government Spokesperson Thanakorn Wangboonkongchana disclosed that Prime Minister and Defense Minister Gen. Prayut Chan-o-cha ordered concerned agencies to be on alert for possible flood and inundation especially in the flood-prone areas, following the 21/2565 announcement of the National Water Command Center on “Alert for Flood-Prone Areas and Flash Flood” due to continued heavy rainfall forecasted during July 21-25, 2022 and possible overflow from rivers and natural water sources in some areas.
The Prime Minister ordered close monitoring of overcapacity reservoirs of which water volume has a tendency to increase, especially in Chiang Mai, Nan, and Phetchabun in the North; Loei, Beung Kan, Khon Kaen, Nakhon Ratchasiman, and Burirum in the Northeast; and Sa Kaeo, and Trat in the East.
As a precaution, concerned agencies are instructed to mobilize integrative operation in closely monitoring daily weather and water situation, clearing possible water obstructions, ensuring that reservoirs and related constructions, as well as river banks, remain intact, and providing immediate assistance to the affected people.

​Ad-hoc committee for economic situation administration established to tackle current economic and energy crisis
July 20, 2022, Government Spokesperson Thanakorn Wangboonkongchana disclosed that Prime Minister and Defense Minister Gen. Prayut Chan-o-cha ordered for the establishment of an ad-hoc committee for economic situation administration to tackle problems related to economic and energy crisis, and consumption products in a bid to alleviate people’s plights.
The Prime Minister signed the 170/2565 order of the Prime Minister’s Office to establish an ad-hoc committee for economic situation administration, which will be chaired by the Prime Minister. Members of the ad-hoc committee include concerned deputy prime ministers (as vice chairs), minister of finance, minister of agriculture and cooperative, minister of transport, minister of interior, minister of industry, permanent secretaries to 7 ministries, secretary general of the Office of Council of State, secretary-general of Office of the National Security Council, secretary general of the Office of National Economic and Social Development Council, director-general of the Budget Bureau, and governor of the Bank of Thailand. The Prime Minister also signed an order, as chair of the ad-hoc committee for economic situation administration, to set up a sub-committee for monitoring and analyzing economic situation with the permanent secretary to Ministry of Finance as chair.
The ad-hoc committee for economic situation administration will identify integrative economic guidelines for concerned agencies to translate into action, prepare response plans, and monitor, follow up, and evaluate implementation of those guidelines and plans, taking into consideration recommendations and comments of the sub-committee for monitoring and analyzing economic situation, to ensure tangible success. The sub-committee’s tasks also include developing response plans to be proposed to the committee.
According to the Government Spokesperson, both the ad-hoc committee for economic situation administration and the sub-committee for monitoring and analyzing economic situation will be the Government’s important tools to tackle and mitigate the impact of global economic crisis in the aspects of trade and investment, energy, product price control, and financial and fiscal policies in a unified and timely manner. At the same time, the Prime Minister has instructed concerned agencies to come up with response plan against energy and food crisis, both at the immediate, short-term, and long-term levels. The Government Spokesperson reaffirmed that the Government commits and strives to address these problems in order to minimize impact against the Thai people and the nation’s economic advancement.

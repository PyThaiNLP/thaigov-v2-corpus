​PM to attend 5th BIMSTEC Summit and to be handed over BIMSTEC chairmanship from Sri Lanka
Government Spokesperson Thanakorn Wangboonkongchana disclosed that Prime Minister and Defense Minister Gen. Prayut Chan-o-cha is scheduled to attend via a teleconference the 5th Bay of Bengal Initiative for Multi-Sectoral Technical and Economic Cooperation (BIMSTEC) and related meetings which will be hosted by Sri Lanka on March 30, 2022. The Sri Lankan President Gotabaya Rajapaksa will deliver an opening speech at 0950hrs the same day.
According to the Government Spokesperson, BIMSTEC is a technical and economic cooperation framework between 7 countries dependent on the Bay of Bengal, namely, Bangladesh, Bhutan, India, Myanmar, Nepal, Sri Lanka, and Thailand. It was first established on June 6, 1997, through the initiative of Thailand who was also host of the 1st BIMSTEC Summit in 2014, to serve Thailand’s “Look West” policy and the “Look East” policy of the South Asian countries, as well as India’s “Act East”.
The 5th BIMSTEC Summit will be held under the theme: “BIMSTEC-Towards a Resilient Region, Prosperous Economies, Healthy Peoples”.
At the 5th BIMSTEC Summit, the Prime Minister will be handed over BIMSTEC chairmanship from Sri Lanka, and deliver a speech. Thailand will chair BIMSTEC for 2 years during 2022-2023. Issues to be put forward during the country’s chairmanship include post-COVID-19 economic recovery, preparation for wide-ranging challenges and crises, and resilience, among others.

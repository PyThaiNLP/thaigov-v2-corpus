Thailand-Indonesia-Malaysia to further forge collaboration under IMT-GT framework
October 22, 2021, Deputy Government Spokesperson Ratchada Thanadirek disclosed about the progress of theIndonesia-Malaysia-Thailand Growth Triangle (IMT-GT), which comprises a number of key projects, such as, 1) Simplification of Customs, Immigration and Quarantine (CIQ) regulations and procedures to enhance cross-border mobility of both the people and goods; 2) Labor market database; 3) E-commerce projects; and 4) Sub-regional physical connectivity with the total value of US$39 billion. As of now, 14 projects have been completed, which include Thailand’s expansion of Hat Yai International Airport (Songkhla province). Projects in progress include Phuket Smart City, and construction of the new phase of Surat Thani and Narathiwat Airports, while a project to be implemented soon is the railroad linking Thailand and Malaysia (Sungai Golok-Rantau Panjang-Tumpat).
Under the IMPT-GT framework, the 3 countries also agreed upon geopark protection. Memorandum of Understanding (MOU) among Langkawi UNESCO Global Geopark, Satun UNESCO Global Geopark, and Toba Caldera UNESCO Global Geopark (IMT – GT Geopark Collaboration in IMT – GT Region) has been signed to promote international cooperation and exchanging experiences of the three countries based on respect for the independence and equality of the geoparks in each member countries.
As for the next phase, IMT–GT plans to have People’s Republic of China, and the Republic of India as IMT–GT’s development partners, and to promote collaboration for the recovery of tourism industry, with the Phuket Sandbox scheme as the model project.

PM pleased with progress of COVID-19 vaccination to young students
January 12, 2022, Government Spokesperson Thanakorn Wangboonkongchana disclosed that Prime Minister and Defense Minister Gen. Prayut Chan-o-cha was pleased with COVID-19 vaccination to students, aged 12-18. According to the report submitted by Ministry of Education as of January 11, 2022, 4,317,337 students, accounting for 83.85% of the total number of students across the country (5,148,710 students), expressed their wish for vaccination, out of whom 4,102,827 (95.03%) received the first shot, and 3,040,726 (70.43%) already received the 2nd shot.
Ministry of Education also plans for the vaccination of children, aged 5-11, and is now collecting the number, and inquiring parents’ consent for approx. 5.2 million students enrolled under the formal system. Vaccination registration for the students under this age group is expected to open by the end of this month. Vaccination will be in the similar manner with the students under the 12-18 year of age group.
Region wise, 97% of the students in the Southern region already received the 1st shot, and 73% received the 2nd shot. 99% of the students in the Northern region already received the 1st shot, and 82% received the 2nd shot. 93% of the students in the Northeastern region already received the 1st shot, and 66% received the 2nd shot. 98% of the students in the Eastern region received the 1st shot, and 75% received the 2nd shot. 99% of the students in the Western region already received the 1st shot, and 76% received the 2nd shot, and 94% of the students in the Central region already received the 1st shot, and 68% received the 2nd shot. The Government will expedite student vaccination to prevent the spread of Omicron, and to ensure their quick return to onsite learning.
As of January 12, a total of 7,681 new confirmed cases is reported on the day, out of which 7,282 were found from the epidemiological surveillance system, 110 from proactive finding, 12 from correctional facilities, and 277 from overseas traveling. The total of 22 deaths and 3,350 recoveries were reported with 66,283 active cases. From January 1, 2022, cumulative number of cases are 68,855, and cumulative number of recoveries, 35,641.
The Government Spokesperson added that the Government also strives to ensure sufficient COVID-19 medical supplies, especially the test kits. Three million kits of the ATKs will be available at all branches of the Government Pharmaceutical Organization from January 17, 2022 onward at the very reasonable price of 35 Baht per kit.

PM to chair full CCSA meeting on March 18
March 14, 2022, Government Spokesperson Thanakorn Wangboonkongchana disclosed that Prime Minister and Defense Minister Gen. Prayut Chan-o-cha is scheduled to chair the full meeting of the Center for COVID-19 Situation Administration (CCSA) on March 18 where the transition into COVID-19 endemic approach, as well as easing of various activities to normalize people’s living, is expected to be proposed by Ministry of Public Health.
Transition to the endemic approach will be divided into 4 stages during the course of 4 months or “3+1”. The first stage, “Combating”, from March 12 to Early April, requires an effort to suppress number of infections and severity through implementation of various measures. The second stage called “Plateau” is set during April and May, which is to maintain the infection rate until it declines. The third stage (end of May – June 30), the “Declining”, requires an effort to curb the number of infections to 1,000-2,000, and the fourth is called “Post-pandemic” (July 1 onward) and a complete transition the endemic approach. In preparing for the transition, the target will be adjusted, and the number of infections will no longer be focused since the situation could change anytime. Instead, vaccination, especially among the 608 groups, will be targeted to reduce deaths and enhance treatment effectiveness.
As of today (March 14, 2022), a total of 22,130 new confirmed cases is reported, among which 22,103 were found domestically, and 27 from overseas traveling. The total of 69 deaths and 23,508 recoveries were reported with 225,889 active cases. From January 1, 2022, cumulative number of cases are 983,520, and cumulative number of recoveries, 788,794. Cumulative number of vaccinations across the country from February 28, 2021 to March 13, 2022 is 126,176,878 doses (1st shot: 54,419,126, 2nd shot: 49,977,239, 3rd shot 19,907,958, and 4th shot: 1,872,555.

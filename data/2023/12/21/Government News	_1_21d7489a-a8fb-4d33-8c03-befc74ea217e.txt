​PM closely monitors earthquake situation in China’s Gansu province
December 20, 2023, Government Spokesperson Chai Wacharonke disclosed that Prime Minister and Minister of Finance Srettha Thavisin has closely followed up on the situation in China’s Gansu province in the wake of the magnitude-6.2 earthquake which struck the northwestern province and nearby provinces during the night of December 18.
As of December 19, 2023, it was reported that the quake has killed at least 127 people and injured several hundreds. This China’s most disastrous earthquake in recent years also greatly damaged buildings, infrastructure and utility system of the province.
According to the Ministry of Foreign Affairs, there is yet a report on any Thai national who was affected by the quake or need immediate help. Nevertheless, if there is any, they may contact the Thai Consulate in Xi’an at phone: 182-0292-1281.
The Government Spokesperson added that the Prime Minister has great concern over Thai nationals residing in the affected area. He ordered relevant agencies to closely monitor and assess the situation in a bid to be prepared and to plan for emergency relief should there be any Thai victim. Those concerned are also urged to keep updated on announcements made by the Ministry of Foreign Affairs.

PM follows up on Bang Yai- Kanchanaburi motorway project, which will open for free from December 28, 2023 - January 3, 2024
Government Spokesperson Chai Wacharoke disclosed that on December 9, 2023, at 15.00 hrs. at Kanchanaburi motorways, Tha Muang District, Kanchanaburi Province, Prime Minister and Minister of Finance Srettha Thavisin followed up on the progress of the intercity motorway construction project, Bang Yai- Kanchanaburi Line (M81), with Mr. Auntin Charnvirakul Deputy Prime Minister and Minister of the Interior, Mr. Suriya Jungrungreangkit Minister of Transport, Mr. Surapong Piyachote Deputy Minister of Transport, and Executives of agencies under the Department of Highways.
Prime Minister listens to a summary of the intercity motorway construction project, the 96-kilometer Bang Yai- Kanchanaburi Line (M81), for which the civil works construction is currently completed on 17 contracts out of a total of 25 (95.962%).  The completed contract is contract number 3 in Bang Yai District, Nonthaburi Province (kilometer 4 + 100 to kilometer 9). The total distance is 4.9 kilometers. The project is a highway between cities with 4 - 6 lanes totaling 96 kilometers, starting connected to Outer Ring Road, Bangkok (Kanchanaphisek Road), Western Bang Yai District, Nonthaburi Province, while at the end of the highway is at Highway No. 324 (Kanchanaburi Road, Phanom Thun District).
The project includes eight tollway gates, which are Bang Yai, Nakhon Chai Si, Sisathong, Eastern Nakhon Pathom, Western Nakhon Pathom, Tha Muang, and Kanchanaburi. Moreover, the construction from the Western Nakhon Pathom to Kanchanaburi distance of 50 kilometers will open for free from December 28, 2023, - January 3, 2024, to reduce the time and number of cars heading to Kanchanaburi Province during the New Year Festival 2024, while the opening of full service will be available in 2025.
The development of the intercity highway network No. 81 Bang Yai- Kanchanaburi will develop and promote Thailand’s road transportation system to be modern, as well as the tourism and regional economic development of the country to create convenience and safety for people to travel to various regions, increase the country’s transportation capabilities, and connect the transportation network to the western and southern regions of the country.

Deputy PM and Public Health Minister to represent Thailand at 76th WHA
May 23, 2023, Deputy Government Spokesperson Traisuree Taisaranakul disclosed that Deputy Prime Minister and Minister of Public Health Anutin Charnvirakul will lead the Thai delegation of Ministry of Public Health executives to attend the 76th World Health Assembly (WHA) in Geneva, the Swiss Confederation during May 23-26, 2023.
The 76th WHA will be held under the theme: “Saving Lives, Driving Health for All”, and will be attended by 194 WHO member countries. WHA’s functions are to determine the policies of WHO, and review and approve the proposed program budget. Agendas of the 76th WHA include public health emergency preparedness and responses, and implementation of the International Health Regulations, as well as global strategy for women’s, children’s and adolescents’ health, universal health coverage, WHO traditional medicine strategy, infection prevention and control, substandard and falsified medical products, health of refugees and migrant promotion, mental health, social determinants of health, nutrition, disabilities, and outlook on financing of the Programme budget 2024–2025, among others.
According to the Deputy Government Spokesperson, other than attending the 76th WHA, the Deputy Prime Minister and Minister of Public Health is also scheduled to have bilateral meetings with representatives of People’s Republic of China, Singapore, Kazakhstan, Bangladesh, and Maldives, and a meeting with WHO Director-General Tedros Adhanom Ghebreyesus to discuss cooperation between Thailand and WHO.

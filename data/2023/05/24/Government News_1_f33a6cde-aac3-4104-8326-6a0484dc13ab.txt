​PM commends progress of Rantau Panjang-Sungai Golok Bridge construction project
May 23, 2023, Deputy Government Spokesperson Ratchada Thanadirek disclosed that Prime Minister and Defense Minister Gen. Prayut Chan-o-cha was pleased with the outcome of the 3rd Malaysia-Thailand joint technical meeting for the Rantau Panjang-Sungai Golok Bridge construction project, and was confident that the bridge would help facilitate connectivity between border special economic zones of the two countries, and promote cross-border transport for passengers and goods, border trade, and tourism. The 3rd Malaysia-Thailand joint technical meeting for the Rantau Panjang-Sungai Golok Bridge construction project was held yesterday (May 22) at Pulse Grand Hotel, Putra Jaya, Malaysia.
The Southern Border Provinces Administration Centre proposed at the meeting that all related problems be solved in time with the budget planning for fiscal year 2024 so that the construction could complete sooner for the benefit of Thai and Malaysian people in Narathiwat province and Malaysia’s Kelantan who have been waiting for the 2nd bridge crossing Sungai Kolok River for over 20 years.
According to the Deputy Government Spokesperson, the Government has a policy to maintain friendly relations with all countries, especially the neighboring countries. The Prime Minister believes that the border situation will continue to improve through friendship and people-to-people exchanges.

PM orders preparation against possible impact of Tropical Cyclone “MOCHA”
PM orders preparation against possible impact of Tropical Cyclone
“MOCHA”
May 15, 2023, Deputy Secretary-General to the Prime Minister and Acting Government
Spokesperson Anucha Burapachaisri disclosed that following the announcement of Thailand
Meteorological Department (TMD) on Tropical Cyclone “MOCHA” No.10, Prime Minister and
Defense Minister Gen. Prayut Chan-o-cha has made an order for concerned agencies to beef up
their preparation against possible impact, and prepare to offer humanitarian assistance should
there be a request.
According to the TMD announcement, at 4.00 a.m. LST on May 15, tropical cyclone MOCHA
covers Sagaing State of Myanmar. Its maximum sustained winds were about 93 km/hr, and was
moving north-northeast. It is, however, expected to further downgrade to the low pressure.
More rains, as well as, isolated heavy rains will be possible in the North.
People should beware of the severe condition that may cause flash floods and overflows. The
strong wind and waves about 2 meters high in the upper Andaman Sea and in thundershowers
more than 2 meters high. The lower Andaman Sea has the waves 1-2 meters high and in
thundershowers more than 2 meters high. All ships should proceed with caution and keep off
thundershowers area. Small boats in the upper Andaman Sea keep ashore.
The Government Spokesperson called on the public, especially those in the likely affected areas,
to closely follow up on the weather update from TMD at http://www.tmd.go.th, or call 0-2399-
4012-13 and 1182, 24/7.

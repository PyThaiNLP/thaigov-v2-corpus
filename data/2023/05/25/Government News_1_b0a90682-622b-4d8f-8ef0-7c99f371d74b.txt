SACIT’s “14 th Legendary of Thai Artisans” showcases
craftsmanship of master artisans and craftsmanship descendants
May 25, 2023, Deputy Secretary-General to the Prime Minister and Acting Government
Spokesperson Anucha Burapachaisri disclosed that Prime Minister and Defense Minister Gen.
Prayut Chan-o-cha expressed support over an organization of the “14 th Legendary of Thai
Artisans” by the SUPPORT Arts and Crafts International Center of Thailand (Public Organization)
or SACIT during May 22-28, 2023 at the Bangkok International Trade &amp; Exhibition Centre
(BITEC). The event is held to honor master craftsmen and artisans, as well as their
descendants, in various fields of arts and crafts, and underscore the importance to preserve skill
and craftsmanship knowledge and local wisdom to pass on to the next generations.
According to the Government Spokesperson, SACIT sought to identify local craftsmen and
artisans, and their descendants based on social parity and fairness in 9 fields of handicrafts, that
is, 1) woodware, 2) basketwork, 3) pottery, 4) textile, 5) lacquerware, 6) ironware, 7)
leatherwork, 8, paperware, and 9) grassware. Selection criteria covers all dimensions prescribed
under the National Strategy, i.e., preservation and inheritance, skill and knowledge, local
wisdoms, contemporary craft and art development, and social dimension. SACIT finally
identified 25 artisans (14 master craftsmen, and 10 descendants) who have craftsmanship in
the making of rare artworks and handicrafts.
The Prime Minister expressed full support on promotion of Thai traditional artworks and
handicrafts through various events and activities. This is to inspire people of the next
generations to preserve, pass on, and take pride in their cultural heritages, while using their
creativity to add socio-cultural, commercial, and economic value through the development of
contemporary arts and crafts.

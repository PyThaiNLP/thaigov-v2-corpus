PM pleased with popularity of Thai food and products in Vietnam
May 8, 2023, Deputy Secretary-General to the Prime Minister and Acting Government Spokesperson Anucha Burapachaisri disclosed that Prime Minister and Defense Minister Gen. Prayut Chan-o-cha was reported on the outcome of an event “Taste of Thailand” held at MM Mega Market, Ho Chi Minh City, Vietnam during April 13-26, 2023, where over 2,400 SKUs of Thai products were offered for sale.
Representatives of Ministry of Commerce and Thai National Shippers' Council visited the event on April 26, and reported that Thai products were very popular among Vietnamese consumers. Other than sales of the Thai products, the event “Taste of Thailand” also featured cooking demonstration of Thai dishes using imported ingredients. Office of International Trade Promotion in Ho Chi Minh City collaborated with MM Mega Market Vietnam to promote potential Thai products, such as Thai food seasonings, beverages, snacks, and fruits, especially sweet tamarinds. The products were well received by Vietnamese consumers.
According to the statistics of Vietnamese Department of Customs, Vietnam imported Thai food and snacks worth USD167.53 million in 2022, an increase of 19% from 2021, which is USD141.31 million. The Prime Minister is pleased with global popularity of Thai products, and is confident that product quality and popularity would well contribute to further expansion of Thai food and product export.

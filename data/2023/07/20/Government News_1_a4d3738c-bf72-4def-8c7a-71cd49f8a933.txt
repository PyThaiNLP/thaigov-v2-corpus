Foreign investment in EEC during first 6 months of 2023 tallies 61 projects worth over 10 billion Baht
July 20, 2023, Deputy Government Spokesperson Tipanan Sirichana disclosed that Prime Minister and Defense Minister Gen. Prayut Chan-o-cha, as chair of the Eastern Economic Corridor Policy Committee (EEC), was pleased with the report of the Department of Business Development on foreign investment in the EEC in the first 6 months of 2023 (January - June 2023), during which 61 investors, accounting for 19% of total investors, is recorded, with the investment value of 10,771 million Baht, or 22% of the total investment value in the EEC.
Among them, 26 were from Japan, with an investment of 4,344 million Baht; 9 from China, with an investment of 752 million Baht; 5 from South Korea, with an investment of 286 million Baht, and 21 others (5,388 million Baht in investment value). These businesses include: 1) consulting services on production process management in various industries; 2) engineering and technical services, such as machinery, equipment, tools and equipment design; 3) industrial machinery and parts manufacturing services; 4) automotive parts manufacturing; and 5) international trade, i.e., procurement of goods, raw materials and parts for domestic wholesale.
The Deputy Government Spokesperson underscored EEC’s potential and recognition among foreign investors. Such success is a result of the Government’s hard work in promoting economic, trade and investment reform in all dimensions. EEC is one of the key projects the Prime Minister has placed great importance on in a bid to drive forward economic development, create more revenues, and increase national competitiveness for better quality of life of the Thai people.

Employment of 68,234 PWDs targeted in 2023
July 10, 2023, Deputy Government Spokesperson Ratchada Thanadirek disclosed about the Government’s success in promoting and improving the quality of life of people with disabilities (PWDs) in all dimensions, especially through employment promotion. In 2022, 63,904 PWDs have been hired by 14,444 companies and organizations nationwide, whereas the Empowerment of Persons With Disabilities Fund has provided loans to support PWDs’ career so that they could make money and be able to support their families. With the Government’s continued commitment to empower PWDs, Ministry of Social Development and Human Security’s Empowerment of Persons With Disabilities Department has set the target for 68,234 PWDs to be employed in 2023.
Under Section 33 of the Empowerment of Persons with Disabilities Act, B.E. 2550, and the amended Empowerment of Persons with Disabilities Act (No. 2), B.E. 2556, which require the Minister of Labor to issue Ministerial Regulations to specify numbers of employees with disabilities that the employers, owners of the establishments and State Agencies shall employ, a Ministerial Regulation was issued, which stipulates that any employer or entrepreneur that has more than one hundred employees shall hire a person with disability who is able to work in any position with a ratio of every one hundred employees without disabilities to one employee with disability. In cases the excess number of employees is more than fifty, an obligation to recruit an additional employee with disability shall be applied.
Section 34 of the Empowerment of Persons with Disabilities Act, B.E. 2550 and the amended Empowerment of Persons with Disabilities Act (No. 2), B.E. 2556 also stipulates that employers or owners of the establishments who do not employ Persons with Disabilities at the proper proportions as prescribed in section 33 shall send money to the Empowerment of Persons With Disabilities Fund, whereas according to Section 35, in the event that any State Agency does not wish to employ Persons with Disabilities for work under section 33 or any employer or owner of the establishment does not employ Persons with Disabilities for work under section 33 and does not wish to send money to the Fund under section 34, the said State Agency, employer or owner of the establishment may grant concessions, arrange places for distributing products or services, hire subcontract employees or hire employment services by special means, provide apprenticeship or equipment or facilities and sign language interpreter or other assistance to Persons with Disabilities or caregiver of Persons with Disabilities based on the criteria, procedures and conditions as prescribed by the Committee in the Rules.

Govt invites public to visit 41st Thailand Tourism Festival (TTF2023) during Aug 2-6
August 4, 2023, Deputy Government Spokesperson Traisulee Taisaranakul disclosed about the overview of Thailand’s tourism situation, the country’s key economic driver as reported by Ministry of Tourism and Sports, which saw continuous increase in the number of incoming tourists and domestic travel of the Thais.
According to the report, during the first 7 months of 2023 (January -July), tourism sector has generated the revenue of over 1,084,575 million Baht, out of which 638,161 million are from foreign tourists. As of July 30, 2023, a number of incoming foreign tourists has amounted to 15,322,175, a leap increase of 384% comparing to the same period of last year, and is expected to reach 25 million by the end of the year as targeted. Visitors from Malaysia (2,439,710), China (1,839,660), South Korea (907,463), India (885,772), and Russia (854,946) make up the top 5 nationalities visiting Thailand.
The Deputy Government Spokesperson added that aside from the ease of COVID-19 situation, various marketing plans and tourism activities, implemented by Tourism Authority of Thailand (TAT) and other concerned agencies to sustainably revive tourism in all dimensions and attract both foreign and Thai tourists, have significantly contributed to tourism growth.
During August 2-6, 2023, TAT will organize “41st Thailand Tourism Festival (TTF2023)” at the Queen Sirikit National Convention Center (QSNCC) to promote Thai tourism in the latter part of the year. The event will focus on promoting unseen and lesser-known tourist attractions in the 5 regions through showcasing cultural identities and local ways of life. TTF2023 features 8 different zones of tourism and wide-ranging activities. The entry to TTF2023 is free of charge. Further information can be inquired at TAT Contact Center 1672 or website: thai.tourismthailand.org/Articles/ttf2023.

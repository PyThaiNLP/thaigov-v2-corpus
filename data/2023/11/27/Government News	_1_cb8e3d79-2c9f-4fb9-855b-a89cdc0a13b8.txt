​PM: Govt opens to TDRI’s constructive recommendations and will take them into consideration
November 24, 2023, at 11.30 hrs, at the Purple Room, Thai Khu Fah Building, Government House, Dr. Somkiat Tangkitvanich, president of the Thailand Development Research and Institute (TDRI), led the TDRI executives to pay a courtesy call on Prime Minister and Minister of Finance Srettha Thavisin to discuss the Government’s research policy. Attending the meeting was Prime Minister’s Secretary General Prommin Lertsuridej. Government Spokesperson Chai Wacharonke disclosed gist of the meeting as follows:
The TDRI president expressed appreciation for the Prime Minister’s hard work in promoting Thailand at the global stage. TDRI, as an institute for public policy researches, would like to propose to the Government a few points of recommendation, that is, 1) public regulatory reform; 2) promotion of creative economy; 3) Thailand’s membership to the Organization for Economic Co-operation and Development (OECD); and 4) preparation on legal mechanism in response to EU’s collection of carbon tariff for imported goods. The TDRI president added that TDRI stands ready to support the Government’s endeavors, and would be pleased to conduct research and study on the issues the Government sees beneficial to the country.
The Prime Minister thanked TDRI for the recommendations, and would assign concerned agencies to take the recommendations into consideration. He understood that TDRI may not agree on all of the Government’s implementing policies, but he assured that the policies are for the people’s best interest. The Prime Minister is also open to constructive comments and recommendations from all sides and sectors

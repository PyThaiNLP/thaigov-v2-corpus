PM pleased with Thailand’s rank in World Happiness Report 2023
March 23, 2023, Deputy Secretary-General to the Prime Minister and Acting Government
Spokesperson Anucha Burapachaisri disclosed that Prime Minister and Defense Minister Gen.
Prayut Chan-o-cha was pleased with Thailand’s rank in the World Happiness Report 2023. The
survey is conducted by UN’s Sustainable Development Solutions Network, and announced on
March 20 each year, which has been established as the annual International Day of Happiness.
The survey was powered by Gallup World Poll who provided World Poll data during 2020-2021.
The data was also obtained from the interview with over 100,000 people in 137 countries, and
evaluated in terms of 6 key variables, namely, social support, GDP per capita, healthy life
expectation, freedom to make life choices, generosity, and freedom from corruption. According
to the survey, Thailand ranks 60 th , 1 notch up from last year, while Finland remains the happiest
country in the world for 6 consecutive years.
The Prime Minister was content that the Government’s effort to implement policies to help out
the people despite various global challenges, coupling with the Thai people’s solidarity and love
to each other and their law-abiding, have contributed to Thailand’s better rank from last year in
the World Happiness Report 2023.

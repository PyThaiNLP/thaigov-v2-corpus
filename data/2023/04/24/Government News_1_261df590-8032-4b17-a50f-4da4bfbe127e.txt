​​ Govt launches LINE: “EES Help Me” to tackle domestic violence
April 21, 2023, Deputy Government Spokesperson Tipanan Sirichana gave an advice to women to make use of the application LINE: “EES Help Me” 24/7 whenever they are physically abused or receive threat of violence. Prime Minister and Defense Minister Gen Prayut Chan-o-cha has placed importance over the issues of child abuse and domestic violence, and pushes for the implementation of the draft Action Plan for Prevention and Solution of Violence Against Children, Women and Family Members, 2023-2027. The Action Plan prescribes implementation of 5Ps, namely, policy, prevention, protection, prosecution, and partnership, to ensure integrative and systematic solutions to the problem. There is also a policy to increase the number of female interrogators and enhance understanding of police officers about the sensitivity of the issue, as suggested by Social Development and Human Security Minister Juti Krairiksh.
The public, especially women and children, can contact LINE: “EES Help Me” 24/7 by sharing location to the authorities for immediate assistance. To add LINE, please click: https://lin.ee/GetbF8D (@esshelpme). After the LINE is added, they can do the following 3 steps:
1) Press the emergency button
2) Share location
3) Fill out contact number for the authorities to call back
The system will immediately get to the nearest police station, and notify the caller to let them know that the police has already receive their report.
According to the Deputy Government Spokesperson, communities and neighbors are urged to inform the authorities should they are aware of any case of domestic violences. They can immediately contact Ministry of Social Development and Human Security’s HOTLINEs 1300, 1387, 191, and 1157 to save the lives of those they may know well.

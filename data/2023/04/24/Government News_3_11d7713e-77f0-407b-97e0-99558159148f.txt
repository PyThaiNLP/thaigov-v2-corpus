Thais in Sudan warned to exercise utmost precaution and avoid traveling to unrest areas
April 20, 2023, Deputy Government Spokesperson Rachada Dhnadirek disclosed about the unrest situation in Sudan which broke out on April 15, 2023 that the Government has closely followed up on the situation. Concerned agencies now stand ready to provide initial relief and assistance. Thai citizens in Sudan are urged to exercise utmost precaution and avoid traveling to unrest areas. They are also advised to hoard food and water in case of emergency, closely update the situation with the Sudanese Government, and comply with the Government’s advisory.
According to the latest update of Ministry of Foreign Affairs, approx 300 Thais are currently in Sudan now. Among whom, around 200 are Muslim students at the International University of Africa in Khartoum, and the rest are either expatriates or spouses of Sudanese citizens. Ministry of Foreign Affairs, through the Consular Department, has approved budget allocation for the procurement of sustenance to be given to the Thai citizens. So far, Thais in Sudan have not been affected by the incident. However, concerned agencies have met to discuss evacuation and assistance plan.
According to the Deputy Spokesperson, currently, evacuation is not possible now due to closure of airport and high risk in domestic traveling. Thai citizens in Sudan are urged to register themselves at: https://docs.google.com/forms/d/e/1FAIpQLSdeAm4HJky1QYVDFppUwJrpa8SIBorXyGVu08-JKDnf7_JgiQ/viewform to receive updates and assistance at the first opportunity.
However, those who need immediate assistance may contact the royal Thai embassy in Cairo 24/7 at+201 0194-01243 or email: consular.cai@mfa.go.th, or the following emergency nos. 096-352-0513, 096-165-7120, 096-352-9015.

​Govt strives to ensure tourists’ safety and elevate quality of life of local people through implementation of transport infrastructure development projects
April 14, 2023, Deputy Secretary-General to the Prime Minister and Acting Government Spokesperson Anucha Burapachaisri disclosed about the rising popularity of Koh Kradan in Trang province among tourists, as accommodations in Koh Kradan during Songkran Holiday are fully booked, following the island being ranked by World Beach Guide, a website based in the United Kingdom, as the best of Top 100 beaches on Earth 2023. The Government, through the Marine Department, has planned to upgrade transport network throughout the country. Trang’s Pak Meng Pier, the departure point for longtail boats going to Koh Kradan, has been renovated and reopened in a ceremony chaired by Prime Minister and Defense Minister Gen. Prayut Chan-o-cha on November 16, 2021.
According to the Government Spokesperson, Pak Meng Pier is not only crucial to Trang’s tourism and transport activities, it is also part of the “Andaman Ring Route” to connect air, land and water traveling in Krabi, Phang Nga and Phuket provinces to generate income to local communities, thereby, contributing to the country’s stability, prosperity and sustainability.
The Government is pleased with positive ranking and review of various Thai tourist destinations, and strives to implement transport infrastructure development projects, taking into consideration environmental aspects, to ensure safety of tourists, and elevate quality of life of local people.

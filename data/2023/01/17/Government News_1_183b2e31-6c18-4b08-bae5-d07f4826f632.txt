​Deputy PM (Anutin Charnvirakul) to attend World Economic Forum and witness signing of MOU on Genomics Cooperation
January 16, 2023, Deputy Government Spokesperson Traisuree Taisaranakul disclosed that Deputy Prime Minister and Minister of Public Health Anutin Charnvirakul is scheduled to lead the Thai delegation of Ministry of Public Health to attend the World Economic Forum (WEF) in Davos Klosters, the Swiss Confederation, and witness the signing of a Memorandum of Understanding on Genomics Cooperation in London, United Kingdom.
World Economic Forum (WEF), which is held during January 16-20, 2023 under theme: "Cooperation in a Fragmented World", will be attended by leaders of government, ministers of finance, trade ministers, ministers of foreign affairs, central bank governors, business sector representatives, and international organizations from all over the world. Apart from the Deputy Prime Minister and the delegation, Thailand will also be represented by Minister of Finance Arkhom Termpittayapaisith.
The Deputy Prime Minister and delegation will attend 2 sessions during January 17 and 18. At the session “Health Systems Transformation”, global cooperation among both public and private sectors on sustainable healthcare system will be discussed, whereas at the session “The Pulling Power of ASEAN”, the Deputy Prime Minister will exchange view with other participants on ASEAN’s role in the midst of intense geoeconomic competition and its contribution to global stability, prosperity, and regional cooperation.
The Deputy Prime Minister will also meet with national leaders to discuss “How to Restart Global Cooperation” in response to such global challenges as climate change, weakened economy, and food and energy crisis, and meet with a number of national representatives and business executives. He will, then, travel to London to witness the signing of a Memorandum of Understanding on Genomics Cooperation between Genomics Thailand and Genomics England, and explore future medical, R&D, and universal healthcare cooperation with UK’s concerned agencies.

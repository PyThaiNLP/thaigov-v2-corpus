Latest update of “Public Health Measures for Foreign Travelers Entering Thailand”
January 11, 2022, Deputy Government Spokesperson Traisuree Taisaranakul disclosed that the Ministry of Public Health’s Department of Disease Control has issued the latest update of “Public Health Measures for Foreign Travelers Entering Thailand” on January 10, 2023, of which the detail is as follows:
Proof of vaccination is no longer required before entering the Kingdom
Passengers traveling from the countries that require negative RT-PCR test results before departure from Thailand (as of Jan 10, 2023: China PRC and India) must have health insurance coverage of at least US$10,000 for COVID-19 treatment that covers the duration of stay in Thailand plus 7 additional days. Quarantine officer will randomly check the proof of insurance of passengers traveling from the listed countries. If the traveler does not have proof of insurance, he/she will have to purchase the health insurance before entering the country. Those with business arrangements, including flight crews and students, may have an official letter from the host or other form of insurance to cover such need.
Holders of Thai passports and transit/transfer passengers are exempted from the health insurance checks.
Airlines must adhere to related regulation and comply with the guidance as appropriate, such as asking passengers to wear mask at all times except during meals or emergency situations.
Passengers who develop symptoms of COVID-19 while on board and on arrival are recommended to get a COVID-19 test upon arrival.
Travelers can contact the Department of Disease Control’s Hotline 1442 or visit https://ddc.moph.go.th/index.php for more information.

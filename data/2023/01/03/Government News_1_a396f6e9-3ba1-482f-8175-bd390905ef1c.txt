​PM orders concerned agencies to beef up safety measures and facilitate people’s traveling back home from New Year holiday
January 2, 2023, Deputy Government Spokesperson Traisuree Taisaranakul disclosed that Prime Minister and Defense Minister Gen. Prayut Chan-o-cha is pleased to witness Thai people’s happiness from being able to visit home and celebrate New Year festivities after the COVID-19 situation eased.
As the New Year holidays is coming to an end, some people have started to travel back. The Prime Minister has instructed concerned agencies, be it, Ministry of Transport, local administrations, local and highway police, to tighten safety measures and facilitate people’s traveling back home from the holiday. He also conveyed his concern over those who will do the driving to have enough rest, check up the vehicle, and abide by the traffic rules. Should they feel tired, they are strongly advised to stop at a rest area provided by local authorities.
As for public vehicles, Ministry of Transport, by the Department of Land Transport, has prepared 209 Checking Points / Rest Areas across the country to ensure that the buses/vans are ready for utmost safety of the passengers. The Prime Minister also expressed concern over waterway transport, especially to and from several islands in the East and the South which are popular tourist destinations, and called on concerned authorities and boat/ferry operators to exercise caution, avoid overloading, and ensure that all the passengers wear life jackets at all time while traveling.
